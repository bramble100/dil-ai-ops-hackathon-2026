## Why Visit?

Amsterdam is one of Europe's most popular destinations, combining world-class museums, a unique canal-laced cityscape, and a famously tolerant culture. It's compact enough to explore on foot or by bicycle, yet packed with enough attractions to fill weeks. **[Multi-source]**

Beyond the capital, the Netherlands offers windmill villages, tulip fields, historic cities, and a unique relationship with water that has shaped the nation's identity for centuries.

## Practical Snapshot

- **Getting around**: 14 tram lines, 30+ bus lines, 5 metro lines, 7 ferries; cycling is king **[Source: amszterdam-latnivalok]**
- **Transport passes**: 24h EUR 10, 48h EUR 16, 72h EUR 21.50, 96h EUR 27.50 **[Source: amszterdam-latnivalok]**
- **I Amsterdam Card**: 1 day EUR 67, 2 days EUR 94, 3 days EUR 115, 4 days EUR 130 -- includes transport + 90 attractions **[Source: amszterdam-latnivalok]**
- **Where to stay**: [Die Port van Cleve](hotels/die-port-van-cleve.md) is a historic option on Dam Square **[Source: port-van-cleve]**
- **Best season**: April--May for tulips; avoid winter unless you like cold + rain **[Source: viajeros-callejeros]**

## Dutch Culture Highlights

The Netherlands is famous for much more than Amsterdam. Key cultural touchstones include:

- **Tulips & flowers**: The national obsession -- [Keukenhof](places/keukenhof.md) is the showpiece **[Multi-source]**
- **Windmills**: From drainage to icon -- see [Kinderdijk](places/kinderdijk.md) and [Zaanse Schans](places/zaanse-schans.md) **[Multi-source]**
- **Cheese**: Gouda, Edam, and Leiden varieties; cheese markets in summer **[Source: viajeros-callejeros, cosas-tipicas]**
- **Cycling**: More bikes than people; flat terrain makes it ideal **[Multi-source]**
- **Water management**: Centuries of fighting the sea -- [Cruquius Museum](places/cruquius-museum.md) tells the story **[Source: cruquius-wikipedia]**
- **Liberal policies**: Regulated approach to soft drugs and prostitution **[Source: top10-amszterdam]**
- **King's Day (Koningsdag)**: April 27 -- the entire country turns orange **[Source: cosas-tipicas]**
