# Getting Around Amsterdam

## Overview

Amsterdam is compact and highly walkable, but has excellent public transport for longer distances. The real king of Amsterdam transport, however, is the bicycle. **[Multi-source]**

## Public Transport

Amsterdam's GVB network includes: **[Source: amszterdam-latnivalok]**

- **14 tram lines** -- the backbone of city transport
- **30+ bus lines**
- **5 metro lines**
- **7 ferry lines** -- free across the IJ river

### Operating Hours
- Day service: 6:00 AM -- 0:30 AM
- Night buses: 0:30 AM -- 7:00 AM (numbered 281-293, all pass through Centraal Station)

| Duration | Price (2026) |
|---|---|

| 2 days | EUR 94 |

## Hop-on Hop-off

CitySightseeing operates combined bus and boat service: **[Source: amszterdam-latnivalok]**

- Bus + boat: EUR 37.50/day (EUR 47.50/2 days)
- Boat only: EUR 29.50/day (EUR 37/2 days)
- Bus only: EUR 29.50/day (EUR 37/2 days)
- Audio guide in 16 languages, free wifi, map, mobile app
- Children under 4 free, ages 4-13 half price

## Walking

The city center is very walkable -- most major attractions are within 30 minutes of each other on foot. The [canal ring](../places/canal-ring.md) is best explored on foot. **[Multi-source]**

## Key Transport Hubs

- **Amsterdam Centraal** -- main train station, tram/bus/metro/ferry hub, national rail connections
- **Museumplein** -- for [Rijksmuseum](../places/rijksmuseum.md), [Van Gogh Museum](../places/van-gogh-museum.md), [Vondelpark](../places/vondelpark.md)
- **Dam Square** -- central tram stop (lines 2, 4, 12, 13, 14, 17, 24) **[Source: amszterdam-nevezetessegek]**
