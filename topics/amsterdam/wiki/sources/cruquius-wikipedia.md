# Museum De Cruquius (Wikipedia)

**Source:** English Wikipedia
**Language:** English
**Coordinates:** 52.33806°N, 4.63833°E

## Summary

Wikipedia article on Museum De Cruquius, a former steam-powered pumping station turned museum in Cruquius, Netherlands. The museum houses what is thought to be the largest steam engine ever built and is a significant industrial heritage site with multiple international designations.

## Historical Background

### The Haarlemmermeer Drainage Project
- Three pumping stations were built to drain the Haarlemmermeer lake: **Leeghwater**, **Lynden**, and **Cruquius**
- Named after **Nicolaas Kruik** (1678-1754), a Dutch surveyor and mapmaker
- The Cruquius station was positioned at the mouth of the **Spaarne river** near **Heemstede**
- A dike was constructed in the **1840s**
- The pump started operation in **1850**
- The lake was fully drained in **3 years**

### Museum History
- Converted to a museum in **1933**

## The Steam Engine

The engine was built by **Harvey & Co of Hayle, Cornwall**, England.

- Thought to be the **largest steam engine ever built**
- Confirmed as the **largest beam engine ever built**
- **Compound steam engine** design:
  - Inner high-pressure (HP) cylinder: **84 inches (2.1 m)** diameter
  - Outer low-pressure (LP) cylinder: **144 inches (3.7 m)** diameter

## Heritage Designations

- **Rijksmonument** (Dutch national monument)
- **ERIH Anchor Point** (European Route of Industrial Heritage)
- **ASME Historic Mechanical Engineering Landmark** (designated 1991, American Society of Mechanical Engineers)

## Nearby Attractions

- **Fort Vijfhuizen** - hosts art exhibitions
- **Fort Cruquius** - part of the **Stelling van Amsterdam** (UNESCO World Heritage Site, Defence Line of Amsterdam)

## Key Takeaways

- A unique engineering museum that tells the story of Dutch water management -- a defining aspect of Netherlands history
- The "largest steam engine ever built" claim makes it a superlative worth highlighting
- The connection to Harvey & Co of Cornwall links Dutch and British industrial heritage
- Nearby UNESCO World Heritage forts (Stelling van Amsterdam) could make this a combined day trip
- Located near Heemstede, accessible from Amsterdam as a half-day excursion
- Multiple international heritage designations underscore its significance
