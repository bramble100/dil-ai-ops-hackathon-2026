# Tulips and Flowers

## The Dutch Flower Obsession

The Netherlands is the world's largest flower exporter, and tulips are the national symbol. The country's love affair with tulips dates back to the 17th century "Tulip Mania" -- one of the first recorded speculative bubbles, when single bulbs traded for more than the price of a house. **[Source: cosas-tipicas]**

## Where to See Flowers

### Keukenhof

[Keukenhof](../places/keukenhof.md) is the world's most famous flower garden, open only in spring. **2026 season: March 19 -- May 10.** Located near Lisse, about 35 km from Amsterdam.

- Early season: crocuses, daffodils, hyacinths, early tulips
- Later season: larger tulip varieties in full bloom
- Pavilions showcase flower displays throughout the season

**[Source: keukenhof]**

### Bloemenmarkt

The [Bloemenmarkt](../places/bloemenmarkt.md) is Amsterdam's floating flower market, open year-round on the Singel canal. Sells flowers, bulbs, and souvenirs. **[Multi-source]**

### Tulip Fields

The fields between Leiden and Den Helder blaze with color from mid-April to early May. Best seen by car or bicycle -- the Bollenstreek (Bulb Region) is the heart of tulip country. **[Source: viajeros-callejeros]**

## Buying Bulbs

- Tulip bulbs make excellent souvenirs (compact, lightweight)
- Buy at [Bloemenmarkt](../places/bloemenmarkt.md) or Keukenhof gift shop
- Check customs regulations for your home country before buying
- Plant in autumn for spring blooming
