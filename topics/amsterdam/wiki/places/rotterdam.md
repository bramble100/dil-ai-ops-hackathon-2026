# Rotterdam

Located 75 km south of Amsterdam, Rotterdam is emphatically NOT a typical Dutch canal city. The city center was devastated by Nazi bombing on May 14, 1940, and was subsequently rebuilt with bold, experimental modern architecture that makes it unlike any other city in the Netherlands. [Source: viajeros-callejeros]

## Overview

Where most Dutch cities preserve centuries of canal houses and church spires, Rotterdam chose a radically different path after its wartime destruction. The result is a city that serves as an open-air showcase of modern and contemporary architecture, attracting design enthusiasts from around the world. [Source: viajeros-callejeros]

## Key Sights

### Modern Architecture
- **Cube Houses (Kubuswoningen)** - Piet Blom's iconic tilted cube dwellings, one of Rotterdam's most photographed landmarks [Source: viajeros-callejeros]
- **Markthal** - a spectacular horseshoe-shaped market hall with apartments arching over an indoor food market, featuring a massive ceiling mural [Source: viajeros-callejeros]
- **Central Station** - Rotterdam's dramatically modern train station, a gateway building that sets the tone for the city [Source: viajeros-callejeros]
- **Erasmus Bridge** - the elegant cable-stayed bridge spanning the Nieuwe Maas river, nicknamed "The Swan" [Source: viajeros-callejeros]
- **Van Nelle Factory** - a UNESCO World Heritage industrial building, a masterpiece of early modern architecture [Source: viajeros-callejeros]

### Surviving Pre-War Buildings
A handful of buildings survived the 1940 bombing and offer a glimpse of pre-war Rotterdam: [Source: viajeros-callejeros]

- **City Hall** - one of the few grand pre-war structures still standing [Source: viajeros-callejeros]
- **Grote or Sint-Laurenskerk** - the only surviving medieval building in the city center [Source: viajeros-callejeros]
- **Witte Huis (White House)** - once Europe's tallest office building when completed in 1898 [Source: viajeros-callejeros]

### Nightlife
- **Witte de Withstraat** - the city's cultural and nightlife artery, lined with galleries, bars, and restaurants [Source: viajeros-callejeros]

## Getting There

### By Train
Take the Intercity Direct from Amsterdam Centraal to Rotterdam Centraal. The journey takes approximately 45 minutes. [Source: viajeros-callejeros]

### By Bus
FlixBus operates a cheaper (though slower) service between Amsterdam and Rotterdam. [Source: viajeros-callejeros]
