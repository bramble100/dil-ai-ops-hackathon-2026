# Volendam & Marken

Two traditional fishing and farming villages located approximately 20 km northeast of Amsterdam, often visited together as a combined day trip. Together they offer a picture of rural Dutch life that contrasts sharply with the bustle of Amsterdam. [Source: viajeros-callejeros]

## Volendam

A picturesque fishing harbor village known for its colorful wooden houses lining the waterfront. The harbor area is the main attraction, with terraces where you can sit and enjoy the view over the IJsselmeer. Volendam has a lively, somewhat touristy atmosphere but retains genuine charm. [Source: viajeros-callejeros]

### Getting to Volendam
Approximately 30 minutes by bus from Amsterdam. [Source: viajeros-callejeros]

## Marken

A quieter, more traditional village compared to Volendam. Formerly an island, Marken is characterized by its traditional houses surrounded by gardens, orchards, and small farms. The pace here is slower and the atmosphere more rural. [Source: viajeros-callejeros]

### Getting to Marken
Reached by ferry from Volendam. Ferry tickets can be pre-booked in advance. [Source: viajeros-callejeros]

## Combining with Edam

The nearby town of Edam, famous worldwide for its eponymous cheese, is a natural addition to a Volendam/Marken day trip. [Source: viajeros-callejeros]

- **Cheese market:** If visiting on a Wednesday during July and August, you can witness a traditional cheese market recreation in Edam [Source: viajeros-callejeros]

## Suggested Day Trip Combinations

A popular full-day itinerary combines all of the following in a single guided tour from Amsterdam: [Source: viajeros-callejeros]

1. [Zaanse Schans](zaanse-schans.md) - windmills and traditional crafts
2. Volendam - fishing harbor
3. Marken - traditional farming village
4. Edam - cheese town

Guided tours covering this circuit are widely available. [Source: viajeros-callejeros]
