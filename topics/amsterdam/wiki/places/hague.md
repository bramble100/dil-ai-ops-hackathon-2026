# The Hague (Den Haag)

The seat of the Dutch government, located 60 km southwest of Amsterdam. While Amsterdam is the constitutional capital of the Netherlands, The Hague is where the country is actually governed, and it serves as a major center of international law and diplomacy. [Source: viajeros-callejeros]

## Overview

The Hague combines political gravitas with cultural richness. It is home to some of the Netherlands' most important institutions and museums, and uniquely among major Dutch cities, it also has its own beach resort. [Source: viajeros-callejeros]

## Key Sights

- **Binnenhof (Parliament)** - the historic complex that has served as the center of Dutch politics since the 13th century [Source: viajeros-callejeros]
- **International Court of Justice** - the principal judicial organ of the United Nations, housed in the Peace Palace [Source: viajeros-callejeros]
- **Mauritshuis** - a world-class art museum best known for housing Vermeer's *Girl with a Pearl Earring* and other Dutch Golden Age masterworks [Source: viajeros-callejeros]
- **Scheveningen** - The Hague's popular beach resort area on the North Sea coast [Source: viajeros-callejeros]

## Getting There

The Hague is easily reachable by train from Amsterdam. It is also commonly included in organized excursions combining it with [Rotterdam](rotterdam.md) and [Delft](delft.md). [Source: viajeros-callejeros]
