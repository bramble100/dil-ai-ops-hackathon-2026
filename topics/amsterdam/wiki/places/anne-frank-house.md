# Anne Frank House

The Anne Frank House is the building on the Prinsengracht where Anne Frank and her family hid from Nazi persecution during World War II. Today it serves as a museum preserving the hiding place and telling the story of the eight people who lived in concealment there. It is one of Amsterdam's most visited and emotionally powerful sites. [Multi-source]

## History

The Frank family fled Germany to the Netherlands when Adolf Hitler came to power in 1933. When the Nazi occupation of the Netherlands made life increasingly dangerous for Jewish families, the Franks went into hiding in July 1942. [Source: amszterdam-nevezetessegek]

The family concealed themselves in the **secret annex** (*Achterhuis*) -- a hidden set of rooms at the back of Otto Frank's business premises on the [Canal Ring](canal-ring.md). For two years (1942--1944), eight people lived in this confined space, sustained by Otto Frank's employees who risked their own lives to bring food and supplies. [Multi-source]

In August 1944, all eight occupants were discovered and arrested. They were deported to concentration camps, ultimately to Auschwitz. Of the eight, only **Otto Frank** -- Anne's father -- survived the war. [Source: amszterdam-nevezetessegek]

Anne Frank's diary, written during the years in hiding, became one of the most widely read accounts of the Holocaust and has been translated into dozens of languages. [Multi-source]

## The Museum

The museum preserves the hiding place as it was when the occupants were discovered. Visitors can see:

- The **secret annex** itself, including the bookcase that concealed the entrance
- **Diary excerpts** and pages from Anne's original writings
- **Photographs** of the Frank family and the other occupants
- **Original objects** from the period of hiding

## Important: Tickets Sell Out

**Online tickets are the only way to visit, and they often sell out weeks in advance.** This is not a museum you can visit on a whim -- plan ahead and book as early as possible, especially during peak season (April--October). [Multi-source]

- Tickets are released in batches; check the official website for release schedules. [Unverified]
