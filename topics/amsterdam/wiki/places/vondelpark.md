# Vondelpark

Vondelpark is Amsterdam's largest and most beloved park, with a history spanning over 150 years. Located near the city center at Museumplein, it is within walking distance of the [Rijksmuseum](rijksmuseum.md) and the [Van Gogh Museum](van-gogh-museum.md). Entry is free. [Multi-source]

## What to Do

### Open-Air Theater

During summer, the park hosts a **free open-air theater** (*Openluchttheater*) with a program of performances including theater, dance, music concerts, and children's shows. It has been a cherished Amsterdam tradition for decades. [Source: amszterdam-latnivalok]

### Children's Activities

The park has multiple **playgrounds** scattered throughout, making it a popular destination for families. [Source: amszterdam-latnivalok]

On **King's Day** (April 27), Vondelpark hosts a special **children's market** (*Vrijmarkt*) where only kids are allowed to trade -- selling and swapping clothes, toys, and other items. It is one of the most endearing King's Day traditions in Amsterdam. [Source: amszterdam-nevezetessegek]

### Picnics and Relaxation

Vondelpark is the city's go-to spot for picnics, sunbathing, reading, and general relaxation. On warm days, it fills with locals and visitors alike spreading out on the lawns. [Multi-source]

## Tips

- Combine a Vondelpark visit with a museum day at Museumplein -- the [Rijksmuseum](rijksmuseum.md) and [Van Gogh Museum](van-gogh-museum.md) are right next door. [Multi-source]
- The open-air theater program is free but popular; arrive early for the best spots. [Unverified]
- The park is lovely for a morning jog or an afternoon stroll year-round, though it truly comes alive in spring and summer. [Unverified]
