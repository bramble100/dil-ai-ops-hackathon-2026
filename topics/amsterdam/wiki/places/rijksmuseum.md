# Rijksmuseum

Amsterdam's largest and most visited museum, the Rijksmuseum houses a collection of approximately 1 million objects, of which around 8,000 are on permanent display. The collection spans 800 years of Dutch and European history, from the medieval period through the Golden Age to the modern era. [Multi-source]

## Location

The Rijksmuseum is situated at Museumplein, Amsterdam's museum quarter, in close proximity to the [Van Gogh Museum](van-gogh-museum.md) and [Vondelpark](vondelpark.md). [Multi-source]

## Collection Highlights

The museum's most celebrated works include:

- **Rembrandt's *The Night Watch*** (1642) -- the crown jewel of the collection and one of the most famous paintings in the world [Multi-source]
- **Vermeer's *The Milkmaid*** (c. 1658) -- an icon of Dutch Golden Age painting [Multi-source]

Beyond its renowned painting galleries, the Rijksmuseum's collection extends well beyond canvas:

- **Drawings and prints** [Source: amszterdam-latnivalok]
- **Photography** [Source: amszterdam-latnivalok]
- **Delft porcelain and ceramics** [Source: amszterdam-latnivalok]
- **Asian art collection** [Source: amszterdam-latnivalok]
- **Museum garden** -- a peaceful outdoor space within the museum grounds [Source: amszterdam-latnivalok]

The collection covers medieval art, the Dutch Golden Age, and modern works, providing a comprehensive sweep of Dutch artistic and cultural achievement. [Multi-source]

## Unique Feature

The Rijksmuseum is reportedly the only museum in the world you can cycle through -- a public tunnel runs underneath the building, allowing cyclists and pedestrians to pass between the two halves of the structure. This is fitting for a city where cycling is a way of life. [Source: amszterdam-latnivalok]

- Allow at least 2--3 hours for a visit; art enthusiasts could easily spend a full day. [Unverified]
