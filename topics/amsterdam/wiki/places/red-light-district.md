# Red Light District (De Wallen)

De Wallen is Amsterdam's famous red-light district, located in the oldest part of the city near [Dam Square](dam-square.md). It is one of the most well-known -- and most visited -- neighborhoods in Amsterdam, attracting tourists with its distinctive neon-lit windows, historic architecture, and vibrant nightlife. [Multi-source]

## The Dutch Approach

The red-light district reflects the Netherlands' distinctive approach to social policy: **legalization and regulation** rather than prohibition. The Dutch philosophy holds that regulating activities like sex work reduces harm, improves safety for workers, and brings the industry under legal oversight. This pragmatic, tolerant stance is a defining feature of Dutch culture. [Source: top10-amszterdam]

The same liberal policy landscape extends to **coffee shops** (cannabis cafes), many of which are found in and around De Wallen. Soft drugs are tolerated under strict conditions -- a policy of *gedoogbeleid* (tolerance) that has been in place since the 1970s. [Source: top10-amszterdam]

## Oude Kerk (Old Church)

Nestled within the red-light district is the **Oude Kerk** (Old Church), Amsterdam's oldest building and oldest parish church. The juxtaposition of a medieval church surrounded by neon-lit windows is one of Amsterdam's most striking contrasts. [Source: amszterdam-nevezetessegek]

## Visitor Information and Etiquette

The area is **safe for tourists** and well-policed, but visitors should observe important rules:

- **Photography of sex workers is strictly prohibited.** This is both a matter of respect and a legal requirement. [Multi-source]
- Be aware of pickpockets, as in any busy tourist area. [Unverified]
- The district is atmospheric both during the day (when the historic architecture is easier to appreciate) and at night (when the red lights create the signature ambiance). [Unverified]

## Tips

- Visit the Oude Kerk for a remarkable contrast between sacred and secular Amsterdam. [Source: amszterdam-nevezetessegek]
- De Wallen is part of Amsterdam's oldest neighborhood and contains many historic buildings beyond its reputation -- look up as you walk through for beautiful gabled facades. [Unverified]
- The area is centrally located and easily reached on foot from [Dam Square](dam-square.md) or Amsterdam Centraal station. [Multi-source]
