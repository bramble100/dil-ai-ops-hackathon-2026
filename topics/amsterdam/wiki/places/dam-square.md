# Dam Square

Dam Square (*De Dam*) is Amsterdam's main square and historic heart. It is the symbolic center of the city and one of the most visited public spaces in the Netherlands. [Multi-source]

## History and Name

The square takes its name from the first dam built on the Amstel river around 1270. This dam -- *Amstel-dam* -- gave the city its name. What began as a practical flood-control structure became the nucleus around which Amsterdam grew into one of Europe's great trading cities. [Source: amszterdam-nevezetessegek]

## Key Buildings and Landmarks

### Royal Palace (Koninklijk Paleis)

The dominant building on the square, the Royal Palace was originally built as Amsterdam's city hall between 1648 and 1655. It became a royal palace in 1808 when **Louis Napoleon** (Napoleon's brother, installed as King of Holland) converted it for his residence. [Source: amszterdam-nevezetessegek]

**Architecture and interior:**
- Designed by **Jacob van Campen**, one of the leading architects of the Dutch Golden Age
- The building rests on **13,659 wooden piles** driven into the marshy ground
- Interior clad in **white marble**
- The **Burgerzaal** (Citizens' Hall) is the centerpiece -- a grand hall featuring a sculpture of Atlas holding the globe
- The palace remains in use for royal receptions and state functions

### Nieuwe Kerk (New Church)

Despite its name, the Nieuwe Kerk dates from **1380--1408**. It serves as the coronation church of the Dutch monarchy -- all investitures of Dutch sovereigns take place here. Today it functions primarily as an exhibition space hosting major cultural exhibitions. [Source: amszterdam-nevezetessegek]

### National Monument

A **22-meter obelisk** on the eastern side of the square, the National Monument commemorates the Dutch victims of World War II and the liberation of the Netherlands. It is the focal point for the annual Remembrance Day ceremony on May 4. [Source: amszterdam-nevezetessegek]

### Other Notable Buildings

- **De Bijenkorf** -- Amsterdam's premier department store, located on the square [Source: amszterdam-nevezetessegek]
- **Grand Hotel Krasnapolsky** -- a historic luxury hotel facing the square [Source: amszterdam-nevezetessegek]

## Getting There

Dam Square is well connected by public transport:

- **Tram lines**: 2, 4, 12, 13, 14, 17, 24 -- alight at the **Dam** stop

[Source: amszterdam-nevezetessegek]

The square is also within easy walking distance of other central Amsterdam attractions, including the [Canal Ring](canal-ring.md), the [Red Light District](red-light-district.md), and the [Begijnhof](begijnhof.md).

## Tips

- The Royal Palace is not open every day; check the schedule before visiting as it closes for state events. [Unverified]
- Dam Square can be very crowded, especially on weekends and during events. Early morning is the quietest time. [Unverified]
- The square is a natural starting point for exploring central Amsterdam on foot. [Multi-source]
