# Delft

A historic city located 60 km southwest of Amsterdam, world-famous for its distinctive blue and white Delft Blue ceramics. With its beautiful small old town surrounded by canals, Delft has been described as looking "like a fairy tale." [Source: viajeros-callejeros]

## Overview

Delft is a university town with a lively student atmosphere layered over centuries of history. The compact old center is ideal for a day of wandering, with canal-side streets, historic churches, and ties to both the Dutch Golden Age painter Vermeer and the Dutch royal family. [Source: viajeros-callejeros]

## Key Sights

- **Market Square (Markt)** - the large central square and heart of the city [Source: viajeros-callejeros]
- **Renaissance Town Hall** - a striking building on the Markt [Source: viajeros-callejeros]
- **Oude Kerk (Old Church)** - one of Delft's two landmark churches [Source: viajeros-callejeros]
- **Nieuwe Kerk tower** - climb for panoramic views over the city [Source: viajeros-callejeros]
- **Vermeer Centre** - dedicated to the life and work of Johannes Vermeer, Delft's most famous resident [Source: viajeros-callejeros]
- **Prinsenhof monastery** - a historic monastery with ties to William of Orange [Source: viajeros-callejeros]

## Delft Blue Ceramics

Delft is synonymous with its iconic blue and white pottery, produced here since the 17th century. The tradition remains central to the city's identity and visiting a ceramics workshop or factory is a highlight of any trip. [Source: viajeros-callejeros]

## Getting There

### By Train
Take a direct train from Amsterdam Centraal to Delft. The journey takes under 1 hour. [Source: viajeros-callejeros]

### By Excursion
Delft is often included in organized day trips combining it with [Rotterdam](rotterdam.md), [The Hague](hague.md), and Madurodam (a miniature park in The Hague). [Source: viajeros-callejeros]
