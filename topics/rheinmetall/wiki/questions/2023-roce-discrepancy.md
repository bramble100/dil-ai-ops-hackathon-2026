# Why does the 2023 ROCE differ between AR 2023 and AR 2025?

## Question

The 2023 ROCE is reported as **19.9%** in the Annual Report 2023 but as **21.4%** in the Annual Report 2025 comparative tables. What accounts for the 1.5 percentage point difference?

## Context

- The AR 2023 states ROCE of 19.9% for FY2023
- The AR 2025 comparative data shows 2023 ROCE as 21.4% — the same figure it reports for 2022
- [Profitability Trends](../financials/profitability-trends.md) uses 21.4% (from AR 2025), which is the latest available figure
- The [extraction notes](../_extraction_notes.md) time-series table shows ROCE: 21.4% for both 2022 and 2023

## Current Thinking

Possible explanations:

1. **Capital employed restatement.** ROCE = Operating Result / Capital Employed. If the denominator was restated (e.g., due to IFRS 5 reclassification of automotive/Power Systems assets), the ratio would change even with the same operating result.
2. **Operating result restatement.** If the 2023 operating result was also restated upward (similar to the 2022 adjustment), the numerator would increase.
3. **Methodology change.** Rheinmetall may have changed what counts as "capital employed" — for instance, excluding discontinued operations retroactively.

The fact that 2022 and 2023 show the identical 21.4% ROCE in the AR 2025 tables is notable and may warrant closer examination.
