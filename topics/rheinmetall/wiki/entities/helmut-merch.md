# Helmut Merch

**Helmut Merch** served as CFO of [Rheinmetall AG](rheinmetall-ag.md) from 2014 through 2024. He was replaced by Klaus Neumann effective 1 January 2025. [Source: AR 2025, p.18]

## Role and Style

Merch was the financial anchor of Rheinmetall's leadership during the transformation years. On earnings calls, he complemented [Papperger's](armin-papperger.md) expansive, operationally-detailed style with:

### Precision and Conservatism
Merch consistently steered analysts toward rigorous financial frameworks. His trademark was counseling patience on cash flow: "I always recommend to evaluate cash flow generation over a two to three year rolling period" -- recognizing the inherent lumpiness of defense milestone payments.

### Balance Sheet Stewardship
Under Merch, Rheinmetall achieved investment grade rating (Moody's) in April 2018 -- a significant milestone. He highlighted this repeatedly as a strategic asset. Equity ratio improved from ~29% (2016) to 38% (2022).

### Key Metrics He Championed
- ROCE as the primary capital efficiency measure
- Cash flow-to-sales ratio (evolved target from 2-4% to 3-5%)
- Contribution margin analysis (he provided the 35% Automotive contribution margin during COVID for analyst modeling)

## Notable Positions

### On Cash Deployment (FY2017)
When asked about the growing cash pile, Merch stated: "We will build up more cash" -- signaling discipline even as Papperger hinted at M&A ambitions.

### On Free Cash Flow Volatility (FY2018)
Operating FCF was -35 MioEUR in 2018, causing analyst concern. Merch calmly explained: a EUR 100 million milestone payment arrived in the first week of January, and EUR 230 million of receivables were collected in the first 10 weeks of 2019. "Please evaluate cash flow generation over a two to three year rolling period."

### On COVID Scenarios (FY2019)
Merch provided the critical modeling parameters: ~35% contribution margin for Automotive, allowing analysts to calculate downside scenarios. He firmly rejected share buyback discussions: "It is not today the time to think about this possible issue."

## Succession

Merch's replacement by Klaus Neumann (previously head of Group Accounting, 12 years at the company) in January 2025 was part of a broader board restructuring that also added a new COO position. The change likely reflects the need for a CFO attuned to the dramatically different financial profile of a pure-play defense company managing EUR 60bn+ in backlog and aggressive capacity investment.
