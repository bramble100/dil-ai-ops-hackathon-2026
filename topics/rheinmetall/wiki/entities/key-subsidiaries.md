# Key Subsidiaries and Joint Ventures

## Major Operating Subsidiaries

### Rheinmetall Waffe Munition GmbH
The main [Weapon and Ammunition](../financials/segment-performance.md) operating entity. External sales EUR 1,872m in 2025 (vs 1,554m in 2024). Responsible for ammunition production across multiple European sites. Operates the largest ammunition factory in Europe at Unterluess (opened September 2025). [Source: Extraction notes]

### Rheinmetall Landsysteme GmbH
Core Vehicle Systems entity covering armored vehicle development and production. Key products: Lynx IFV, Leopard 2 components, Puma (via PSM JV), and logistics vehicles.

### Rheinmetall Electronics GmbH
Electronic Solutions operating entity covering sensors, C4ISR, simulation, and training systems.

### Rheinmetall Technology Center GmbH (RTC)
Cross-divisional R&D center focusing on "innovative technologies and products, also outside the existing product portfolio." Part of the Cluster New Technologies alongside MIRA GmbH and Yardstick Robotics GmbH. [Source: AR 2025, p.30]

## Joint Ventures and Partnerships

### RBSL (Rheinmetall BAE Systems Land)
- **Ownership:** 55% Rheinmetall, 45% BAE Systems
- **Formed:** 2019 (Rheinmetall acquired 55% of BAE's UK land systems business)
- **Purpose:** UK armored vehicle manufacturing, Challenger 2 LEP, Boxer production for British Army
- **Significance:** Established Rheinmetall as a major UK defense prime, providing production capacity and engineering know-how. [Source: Transcript FY18]

### Leonardo JV (LRMV)
- **Purpose:** Joint venture with Leonardo for Italian MBT (Panther) and IFV (Lynx)
- **Context:** Part of European defense consolidation efforts in ground combat systems
- **Status:** Active as of 2025, described as "a sign of European consolidation in land systems" [Source: AR 2025, p.9]

### PSM (Projekt System & Management GmbH)
- **Ownership:** JV with Krauss-Maffei Wegmann (KMW)
- **Purpose:** Puma IFV program management for the Bundeswehr
- **Notable 2025:** 200 Puma IFVs ordered by the Bundeswehr via PSM [Source: Extraction notes]

### MGCS Project Company
- **Partners:** KNDS DE, KNDS FR, Rheinmetall, Thales
- **Purpose:** Main Ground Combat System development -- the Franco-German next-generation MBT program
- **History:** Discussed since 2018-2019 at government level. Papperger referenced active Franco-German discussions repeatedly on earnings calls. Project company formally founded in 2025. [Source: Extraction notes, Transcript FY18]
- **Significance:** The largest ground combat procurement program in Europe's future.

### Rheinmetall MAN Military Vehicles (RMMV)
- **User Nations:** 12 nations in the Rheinmetall MAN User Nations Group
- **Products:** Military trucks and logistics vehicles

## Recent M&A (2024-2026)

| Date | Target | Significance |
|------|--------|-------------|
| 2024 | LOC Performance (US) | US market entry for key vehicle components |
| April 2025 | Hagedorn | Nitrocellulose producer -- vertically integrating ammunition raw material supply |
| Feb 2026 | NVL B.V. (Naval Vessels Lurssen) | Entry into naval defense -- post-balance-sheet event |

[Source: Extraction notes]

The NVL acquisition is strategically significant: it adds a naval dimension to Rheinmetall's previously land/air-focused portfolio, creating a more complete defense group.

### M&A Philosophy

Papperger's consistent stance on M&A across all transcripts was disciplined: "We only do such a deal if it makes sense for us... we are not under pressure." (FY2017 call). At the same time, he maintained an active pipeline: "We are looking for 30 to 50 different companies, which really fit to us." (FY2020 call)

## F-35 Partnership
Rheinmetall started F-35 fuselage mid-section production at Weeze in 2025 in partnership with Northrop Grumman/Lockheed Martin -- a significant entry into military aviation manufacturing. [Source: Extraction notes]
