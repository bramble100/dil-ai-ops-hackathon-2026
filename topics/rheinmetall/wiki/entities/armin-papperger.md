# Armin Papperger

**Armin Papperger** has served as CEO (Vorstandsvorsitzender) of [Rheinmetall AG](rheinmetall-ag.md) since 1 January 2013. His contract was renewed in January 2025 for a further five years (through 2029), reflecting the supervisory board's confidence in his leadership through the company's most transformative period. [Source: AR 2025, p.18]

## Profile

Papperger is the architect of Rheinmetall's transformation from a mid-tier defense/automotive conglomerate into one of Europe's leading pure-play defense companies. He presided over:

- Revenue growth from ~5.9bn (2017) to ~9.9bn (2025) EUR
- Operating margin expansion from 6.8% to 18.5%
- The strategic [pivot from automotive to pure defense](../concepts/automotive-to-defense-pivot.md)
- Navigation through COVID-19, the Ukraine crisis, and European rearmament

## Leadership Style (from Earnings Call Transcripts)

The earnings call transcripts reveal Papperger as:

### Operationally Hands-On
He routinely provides granular detail on specific deals, customer relationships, and production challenges. On the FY2019 call, during early COVID, he described "Corona action teams" and how blue-collar workers were organized into isolated groups: "If there is an infection of one group, we can carry on with work."

He revealed unusually deep operational awareness, noting Rheinmetall held one year of nitrocellulose stock as ammunition raw material: "This is the reason of our risk management that we have also higher working capital." [Source: Transcript FY19]

### Politically Connected
Papperger's personal relationships with government officials are a recurring theme. He frequently references direct conversations with defense ministers, procurement chiefs, and heads of state. On the FY2017 call, he cited the Munich Security Conference as evidence of a European defense awakening: "It's the first time that the European ministers told that Europe must be stronger."

### Conservative on Guidance, Bullish on Vision
A consistent pattern emerges: Papperger sets conservative financial targets and then beats them. On the FY2020 call: "Nothing is wrong. But you know that we are really conservative. We want to deliver what is going into the guidance and we want to deliver 100%. And as you know year by year, at the end of the year, usually we deliver more."

At the same time, his long-term vision was consistently bold -- calling the "defense super cycle" as early as FY2018 and positioning Rheinmetall as a European defense champion.

### Disciplined on M&A
Despite active pipeline ("We are looking for 30 to 50 different companies"), Papperger consistently resisted pressure to make acquisitions for their own sake: "We only do such a deal if it makes sense for us... we are not under pressure." [Source: Transcript FY17]

## Key Quotes

> "As you may recall from our Capital Markets Day, we spoke of the beginning of a super cycle in the Defence business and 2018 marked the beginning of this cycle." (FY2018 call, March 2019)

> "If you have a crisis usually defense is more important than if you have no crisis." (FY2019 call, March 2020 -- on whether COVID fiscal pressures would hit defense budgets)

> "We are seen as a full auto company." (FY2019 call -- frustration at market perception)

> "The goal is to create stronger emphasis to Defence and to reduce our dependency from internal combustion engines." (FY2020 call -- the pivotal transformation statement)

> "As long as Germany is in the NATO, it doesn't matter which coalition is really ruling this country." (FY2020 call -- on German election risks)
