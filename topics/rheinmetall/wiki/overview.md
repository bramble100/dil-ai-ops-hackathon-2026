# Rheinmetall AG

**Rheinmetall AG** is a German defense and security technology group headquartered at Rheinmetall Platz 1, Dusseldorf, Germany. Listed on the Frankfurt Stock Exchange (XETRA: RHM), it is a member of the DAX index and one of Europe's largest defense contractors.

## Corporate Structure (2025)

As of late 2025, Rheinmetall reports through three defense-focused divisions:

1. **Vehicle Systems** (Europe + International) - Armored vehicles, logistics vehicles, turret systems. Includes the Lynx IFV family and legacy Leopard 2 support.
2. **Weapon and Ammunition** - Weapons, ammunition, air defense, force protection. Key growth driver in the Ukraine era.
3. **Electronic Solutions** - Sensors, C4ISR, cyber defense, simulation and training. The digitalization/AI arm.

A fourth segment, **Power Systems** (civilian automotive components), was classified as "discontinued operations" (nicht fortgefuhrte Aktivitaeten) under IFRS 5 effective 17 December 2025. [Source: AR 2025, p.5] This marks the culmination of a years-long [pivot from automotive to pure defense](concepts/automotive-to-defense-pivot.md).

## Historical Trajectory

Rheinmetall's story from 2017-2025 is one of **radical transformation** driven by geopolitical events:

| Phase | Period | Defining Event | Revenue Range |
|---|---|---|---|
| Dual-segment conglomerate | 2017-2019 | Legacy defense + automotive | 5,400-6,300 MioEUR |
| COVID disruption | 2020-2021 | Pandemic hits automotive hard, defense resilient | 5,400-5,700 MioEUR |
| Zeitenwende inflection | 2022-2023 | Russia invades Ukraine (Feb 2022), European rearmament begins | 6,400-7,200 MioEUR |
| Defense champion | 2024-2025 | Pure defense focus, automotive divested | 7,700-9,900 MioEUR |

The explosion in order intake from 5.6bn (2022) to 19.9bn (2025) -- a 3.5x increase in three years -- reflects the [European defense spending boom](concepts/european-defense-spending.md) triggered by the [Ukraine war](concepts/ukraine-war-impact.md).
