## The Margin Story

Rheinmetall's operating margin nearly tripled from 6.3% (2016) to 18.5% (2025). This is exceptional for a defense company and reflects multiple converging factors:

### 1. Mix Shift Toward Defense
Defense margins have historically been higher than automotive. As the [automotive-to-defense pivot](../concepts/automotive-to-defense-pivot.md) progressed, the overall margin mechanically improved. The classification of Power Systems as discontinued operations in late 2025 removed the dilutive effect entirely.

### 2. Operating Leverage on Volume Growth
Revenue grew faster than costs. Defense manufacturing has high fixed costs (R&D, tooling, facilities) that don't scale linearly with output. The [ammunition capacity expansion](../concepts/ammunition-capacity-expansion.md) and other investments began paying back as utilization rates rose.

### 3. Pricing Power in a Sellers' Market
Post-Ukraine, European armies needed equipment urgently and Rheinmetall was one of few suppliers capable of delivering at scale. This structural demand imbalance likely supported pricing.

### 4. Scale Benefits on Ammunition Production
Ammunition is a high-volume, relatively margin-rich product line. The massive ramp-up in [Weapon and Ammunition](segment-performance.md) production drove disproportionate profit contribution.

## ROCE: The Capital Efficiency Story

ROCE expanded from 12.3% (2016) to 33.5% (2025) -- nearly 3x. This means Rheinmetall generated EUR 33.50 of operating profit for every EUR 100 of capital employed, an extraordinary return for a capital-intensive industrial company.

## Net Income: The Automotive Drag

| Year | Net Income (MioEUR) | Continuing Ops (MioEUR) | Discontinued Ops (MioEUR) |
|------|--------------------|-----------------------|--------------------------|
| 2020 | 1 | 284 | -283 |
| 2021 | 332 | 432 | -100 |
| 2022 | 540 | 534 | 6 |
| 2023 | 586 | 630 | -44 |
| 2024 | 808 | 840 | -32 |
| 2025 | 835 | 1,176 | -341 |

[Source: AR 2025 p.3]

**2020 is the extreme case:** The company earned 284 MioEUR from continuing defense operations but lost 283 MioEUR on discontinued automotive, resulting in net income of just 1 MioEUR. This crystallizes why the strategic pivot was necessary.

**2025 is notable:** Despite record operating performance, net income (835) was depressed by a -341 MioEUR charge from discontinued operations -- likely the final write-down/separation costs for divesting Power Systems.
