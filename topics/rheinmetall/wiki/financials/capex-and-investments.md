# Capital Expenditure and Investments

## Capex Time Series

| Year | Capex (MioEUR) | As % of Revenue | Context |
|------|---------------|----------------|---------|
| 2017 | -270 | 4.6% | Normal maintenance + growth |
| 2018 | -277 | 4.5% | Australia MilVehCoE beginning |
| 2019 | -288 | 4.6% | Steady investment |
| 2020 | -237 | 4.4% | COVID restraint |
| 2021 | -271 | 4.8% | Recovery begins |
| 2022 | -349 | 5.4% | Initial capacity response to Ukraine |

[Source: Extraction notes]

## The 2024-2025 Capex Inflection

Capex more than doubled between 2023 (398m) and 2024 (697m), then rose a further 25% to 872m in 2025. As a percentage of revenue, it jumped from ~5% to ~9% -- signaling a fundamental shift from incremental investment to capacity transformation.

## Investment by Segment (2025)

| Segment | Investment (MioEUR) | Share (%) | Revenue (MioEUR) | Capex/Revenue |
|---------|--------------------|----|-----------------|--------------|
| Vehicle Systems | 199 | 23% | 4,992 | 4.0% |
| Weapon and Ammunition | **402** | **46%** | 3,532 | 11.4% |
| Electronic Solutions | 174 | 20% | 2,504 | 6.9% |
| Other/consolidation | 101 | 12% | - | - |
| **Total (continuing)** | **876** | 100% | 9,935 | 8.8% |

[Source: Extraction notes]

**Weapon and Ammunition dominates capex** at 46% of total investment despite generating 36% of revenue. At 11.4% of segment revenue, this is a massive build-out -- reflecting the [ammunition capacity expansion](../concepts/ammunition-capacity-expansion.md) program. Key investments include:
- Largest ammunition factory in Europe opened in Unterluess (September 2025)
- Raketenartillerie (rocket artillery) factory planned at Unterluess
- Hagedorn nitrocellulose acquisition (vertical integration)
- Ukraine production facilities

## R&D Investment (Continuing Operations)

|------|---------------------|----------------|
| 2024 | 325 | 4.2% |
| 2025 | 401 | 4.0% |

R&D by segment (2025): Vehicle Systems 256m, W&A 78m, Electronic Solutions 56m. [Source: Extraction notes]

R&D employees grew from 4,816 (2024) to 5,205 (2025) in continuing operations -- a +8% increase.

## Free Cash Flow Despite Heavy Investment

Despite record capex, Rheinmetall generated record FCF:

| Year | Op. FCF (MioEUR) | Capex (MioEUR) | CF from Ops (MioEUR) |
|------|-----------------|----------------|---------------------|
| 2023 | 344 | -398 | 743 |
| 2024 | 1,023 | -697 | 1,720 |
| 2025 | 1,415 | -872 | 2,287 |

Cash from operations grew even faster than capex, allowing the company to invest aggressively while generating substantial free cash. The cash conversion rate (FCF/Op. Result) was 66% in 2025. [Source: Extraction notes]
