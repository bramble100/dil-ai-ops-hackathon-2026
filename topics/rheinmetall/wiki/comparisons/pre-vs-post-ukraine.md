# Pre vs Post Ukraine: The Inflection Point

Russia's invasion of Ukraine on 24 February 2022 was the defining event in Rheinmetall's modern history. This page quantifies the before-and-after across every major financial and strategic dimension.

## Qualitative Shifts

### Before (2017-2021)
- **Market perception:** "We are seen as a full auto company" -- [Papperger](../entities/armin-papperger.md), March 2020
- **Stock price:** EUR 83 (end 2021) -- essentially flat vs. 2017 (EUR 106)
- **Index:** Mid-cap MDAX
- **Strategy:** Dual-segment, cautious defense growth, automotive headwinds
- **Political environment:** Defense spending politically contentious in Germany; ESG skepticism toward defense
- **Order intake metrics:** Not separately reported in current framework

### After (2022-2025)
- **Market perception:** European defense champion, DAX 40 member (2023), EURO STOXX 50 (2025)
- **Stock price:** EUR 186 (end 2022), likely significantly higher by 2025 [Gap: 2025 year-end price not in corpus]
- **Strategy:** Pure defense focus, automotive divested
- **Political environment:** Defense spending is political consensus; Rheinmetall seen as strategically critical
- **Supervisory board:** Former Foreign Minister Sigmar Gabriel appointed (May 2025)
- **2030 target:** EUR ~50bn revenue, >20% margin [Source: Extraction notes]

## Key Insight

The data reveals that the Ukraine war didn't just accelerate an existing trend -- it created a **phase change**. Before 2022, Rheinmetall was slowly growing defense margins while managing automotive decline. After 2022, it became one of Europe's fastest-growing industrial companies with unprecedented order visibility.

The most dramatic metric: order backlog grew from ~13bn to ~64bn -- a **5x increase** that provides visibility through the end of the decade.

## Was This All Ukraine?

Not entirely. Three factors predated the invasion:
1. **Legacy contract phase-out** (Puma, Boxer) was already improving margins structurally
2. **ONE Rheinmetall** restructuring (Feb 2021) was planned before the invasion
3. **Papperger's "super cycle" call** (2018) anticipated growing defense budgets

But the scale and speed of the transformation was overwhelmingly driven by post-Ukraine rearmament. Without the invasion, Rheinmetall would likely be a ~EUR 7-8bn company with ~12% margins today. [Analysis]
