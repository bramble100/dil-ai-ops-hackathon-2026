# Ukraine War Impact

## The Zeitenwende Moment

On 24 February 2022, Russia invaded Ukraine. Three days later, German Chancellor Olaf Scholz declared a "Zeitenwende" (turning point) in German security policy, announcing a EUR 100 billion special defense fund (Sondervermoegen) and committing to the [NATO 2% GDP target](european-defense-spending.md).

For Rheinmetall, this was the most consequential external event in the company's modern history. Within months, the company went from a modestly growing defense contractor to the centerpiece of Europe's rearmament effort.

## Channels of Impact

### 1. Direct Ukraine Support
Rheinmetall became a key supplier to the Ukrainian Armed Forces, delivering:
- Ammunition (artillery shells, tank ammunition)
- Armored vehicles (refurbished Leopard 1/2, Marder IFVs)
- Air defense systems
- Maintenance and support services

The company established production facilities in Ukraine itself -- a bold strategic move referenced in the supervisory board report. [Source: AR 2025, p.9: "neue Werke in enger Abstimmung mit auslaendischen Regierungen, wie z.B. in der Ukraine"]

### 2. European Rearmament
The broader and more durable impact is Europe-wide rearmament:
- **Germany:** EUR 100bn Sondervermoegen, commitment to 2% NATO target (later raised to 3.5% goal)
- **Poland, Baltic states, Nordics:** Massive defense spending increases
- **EU level:** European Defence Industrial Strategy, joint procurement initiatives

### 3. Capacity Expansion
To meet demand, Rheinmetall invested aggressively in new production capacity:
- [Ammunition factories](ammunition-capacity-expansion.md) across Europe
- New armored vehicle assembly lines
- Factory in Lithuania
- Joint ventures (Leonardo JV for ground combat systems)

### 4. Accelerated Strategic Pivot
The war provided the final impetus for the [automotive-to-defense pivot](automotive-to-defense-pivot.md). With defense demand seemingly unlimited and capacity-constrained, investing in automotive made no strategic sense.

## Board Statement (2025)

From the Vorstand Statement (translated):

> "The Russian war of aggression in Ukraine continues. New crisis hotspots have emerged. Rheinmetall continues to take responsibility in this changing world. [...] Europe must close the security gap created by the reorientation of the USA on its own and take its fate into its own hands, not least with regard to its own security and defense capability."

[Source: AR 2025, p.8]

The reference to "the reorientation of the USA" is significant -- by late 2025, European defense independence had become a primary strategic theme, extending the Zeitenwende beyond Ukraine into a broader transatlantic realignment. [Analysis]

## Supervisory Board Perspective

The Aufsichtsrat report notes the geopolitical context shaped all major board discussions in 2025:

> "We are confronted not only with the ongoing war in Ukraine and new crisis hotspots, particularly in the Middle East, but also with social upheavals, ecological challenges, and a changing world order."

[Source: AR 2025, p.10]
