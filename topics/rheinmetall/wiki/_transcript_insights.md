# Rheinmetall AG -- Earnings Call Transcript Insights (FY2017--FY2020)

Primary source extraction from investor relations conference calls featuring CEO Armin Papperger and CFO Helmut Merch. These transcripts capture how management framed strategy, performance, and outlook at each point in time.

---

## FY2017 Results (Call: 15 March 2018)

### Key Management Statements

**Papperger** opened by declaring 2017 "a very successful year for Rheinmetall." Group sales rose 5.2% to EUR 5.9 billion, operating result improved 13.3% to EUR 400 million, and cash flow surged more than 70% to EUR 276 million. He emphasized the cash conversion rate rising from 46% to 69%.

**Merch** highlighted a "great step forward" in balance sheet strength: net cash position grew by EUR 211 million to EUR 230 million, equity ratio rose from 29% to nearly 32%, and Moody's upgraded the outlook to positive (Ba1). He noted Rheinmetall earned its cost of capital with ROCE expanding 110 basis points to 13.4%.

Papperger was confident about internationalization: "We achieved further success in the perception of Rheinmetall as a systems provider." Partnership agreements were signed with Rohde und Schwarz, Raytheon, and Lockheed Martin.

### Guidance Given

- **Group 2018 sales growth**: 8% to 9%
- **Automotive 2018 sales growth**: 3% to 4% (targeting 100-200 bps market outperformance)
- **Defence 2018 sales growth**: 12% to 14%
- **Automotive operating margin**: ~8.5%
- **Defence operating margin**: 6% to 6.5%
- **Group operating margin**: ~7%
- **Holding expenses**: ~EUR 30 million
- **Group tax rate**: below 30%
- **Cash flow to sales ratio strategic target**: 2% to 4% for both segments
- **Net interest**: expected flat at ~EUR 39 million in 2018

### Strategic Commentary

**Automotive strategy (5 pillars)**:
1. Increase content per car (higher complexity for hybrids, fuel efficiency)
2. Develop powertrain neutrality toward e-mobility
3. Enter new markets leveraging existing expertise
4. Grow position with Chinese OEMs (single biggest market)
5. Target 50% electrified products by end of 2020 -- "already well on track"

**Defence strategy**:
- Growing military spend worldwide, especially Germany
- Multiple vehicle tenders approaching final stage (Australia, Hungary)
- Joint venture strategy in Eastern Europe (Romania, Poland)
- Improving earnings quality as legacy contracts fade out; order book margin increasing
- Positioning for future main battle tank and indirect fire systems -- "big potential for Rheinmetall"

**Australia Land 400** was a landmark moment. Papperger: "The nomination of Rheinmetall Defence as the preferred bidder for the Australian Land 400 program represents a new milestone in our company's history." He described low upfront investment risk (Queensland government building the factory, Rheinmetall renting) and a risk-reduction strategy of training Australian workers in Germany.

**M&A**: Papperger stated clearly: "We will need it for the European consolidation for sure and something will happen." But he also noted: "We are not under pressure" -- organic growth was strong enough. He was looking in both Defence and Automotive.

**European consolidation theme**: Papperger referenced the Munich Security Conference, noting "it's the first time that the European ministers told that Europe must be stronger and must be a counterpart also to the United States of America. This is only possible if we have a governmental strategy and then industrial strategy. And both of them are on the way."

### Division Performance

**Automotive (sales EUR 2.9 billion, +7.7%, margin 8.7%)**:
- Mechatronics was the main growth driver (emissions/fuel efficiency products), achieving a record Q4 margin of 11.3%
- Hardparts: sales grew but profitability suffered from ramp-up issues at Neckarsulm JV and EEG discontinuation
- Aftermarket: operating results up 33%, benefiting from Eastern European demand and reduced Czech ramp-up costs
- China: sales ~EUR 1 billion, outperforming market; JV margin expanded to 7.7%, 100% subsidiary margin to 8.9%
- E-mobility order backlog: EUR 500 million in nomination letters
- Outperformed global LV production growth in every single region

**Defence (sales +3.1%, margin 5.7%)**:
- Growth below original expectations due to air defense contract shifts, delayed order intakes, and delayed German government formation
- Operating result rose from EUR 147 million to EUR 174 million
- Operating free cash flow more than doubled to EUR 238 million
- Weapon & Ammunition: sales +10%, margin improved from 16.1% to 17.9%
- Electronic Solutions: sales flat, result declined 24% (burdened by preparation costs for strategic projects)
- Vehicle Systems: best Q4 in sales and profit
- German order intake: EUR 1.6 billion (slightly exceeding ~EUR 1.5 billion guidance)

### Analyst Concerns

1. **Automotive margin guidance decline** (Treisch, MainFirst): Why guide 8.5% vs. 8.7% achieved? Merch said this was midterm guidance and minor adjustments possible with tailwinds.
2. **Land 400 risks** (Tusa, Agency Partners): Worried about development risk for an "extremely advanced vehicle" with an "incredibly demanding customer." Papperger reassured: turret (Lance) in good condition, vehicle in serial production in Germany, risk reduction via dual German-Australian programs.
3. **M&A risk** (Schramm, HSBC): Pushed back on M&A saying "at the moment you have such strong organic growth to handle. M&A I think would be more risk than a chance." Papperger agreed they were "not under pressure" and would only do deals that "make sense."
4. **Cash usage** (Treisch, MainFirst): Asked about EUR 230 million net cash. Merch: "We will build up more cash." Papperger: "We will need it for the European consolidation."
5. **Defence division growth split** (Weier, UBS): Merch clarified Electronic Solutions would show highest growth in 2018, followed by Vehicle Systems; Weapon & Ammunition flat temporarily.

### Forward-Looking Statements

- Defence "super cycle" beginning, supported by growing military budgets globally
- Germany expected to push toward 100% equipment levels
- EUR 2.8 billion order backlog for 2018 alone, giving confidence in sales targets
- Next-generation weapon systems discussions started (future MBT, indirect fire)
- German Boxer order of ~70 vehicles possible (same configuration as Australia)
- Netherlands potentially ordering 40 additional vehicles
- UK decision on Boxer expected "very soon"
- Sikorsky helicopter partnership: EUR 5.5 billion procurement + ~EUR 2 billion maintenance over 10 years

### Notable Quotes

> "We will need it for the European consolidation for sure and something will happen." -- Papperger on cash reserves and M&A

> "We only do such a deal if it makes sense for us... we are not under pressure." -- Papperger on M&A discipline

> "It's the first time that the European ministers told that Europe must be stronger and must be a counterpart also to the United States of America." -- Papperger on European defence awakening

> "The nomination of Rheinmetall Defence as the preferred bidder for the Australian Land 400 program represents a new milestone in our company's history." -- Papperger

## FY2018 Results (Call: 13 March 2019)

### Key Management Statements

Papperger described 2018 as "an extraordinary year for Rheinmetall from our financial perspective." Sales rose to EUR 6.1 billion, operating result to EUR 492 million, EPS up 36% to EUR 7.10. The dividend proposal increased to EUR 2.10 (+EUR 0.40).

But the single most important metric for Papperger was **order intake of EUR 8.5 billion**: "It shows that our efforts to offer a competitive product portfolio has clearly been rewarded by our customers and presents a solid base for our future development."

He explicitly invoked the "super cycle" concept: "As you may recall from our Capital Markets Day, we spoke of the beginning of a super cycle in the Defence business and 2018 marked the beginning of this cycle."

The call was overshadowed by the "tragic incident" at the South African Somerset West site where eight employees died: "The loss of eight colleagues has been a very distressing experience."

**Merch** highlighted the balance sheet: equity ratio improved to 32%, investment grade rating returned in April 2018, and ROCE expanded to 17.1%.

### Guidance Given

- **Group 2019 sales growth**: 4% to 6% (at constant FX)
- **Automotive 2019 sales**: 0% to 1% growth
- **Defence 2019 sales**: 9% to 11% growth
- **Group operating margin**: ~8% (including holding expenses of EUR 25-30 million)
- **Automotive operating margin**: ~8% (adjusted down from prior midterm guidance)
- **Defence operating margin**: 8% to 8.5%
- **Midterm Automotive margin**: adjusted to ~8% (lower end of old guidance)
- **Midterm Defence margin**: raised by 200 bps to 8% to 9%
- **Defence capex guidance**: 3.5% to 4.5% of sales
- **Defence R&D**: 2% to 2.5% of sales (self-financed)
- **Defence ordinary order intake**: EUR 4-6 billion p.a. over next three years
- **Automotive Q1 2019**: expected ~minus 5% sales decline, ~6% margin

### Strategic Commentary

**Automotive resilience narrative**: Papperger acknowledged headwinds (diesel discussion, WLTP, tariffs, Brexit) but stressed: "Management action to tackle and make our cost structure more flexible as early as possible definitely paid off" -- record operating margin of 8.9% for the full year despite turbulence.

However, the outlook was cautious: "We have a relatively high exposure to regions predicted with negative growth. 80% of our sales go to Europe and North America." He was guiding for "negative or at least stagnating market growth" as a base case.

**Defence super cycle deepening**: Record order intake of EUR 5.5 billion. The two key 2018 contracts were Australia Land 400 Phase 2 and Australian truck contract extension (~EUR 2.6 billion combined). "Almost three-quarters of the order intake came from our so-called home market" -- substantially de-risking the backlog.

**Legacy contracts phasing out**: The Dutch Boxer and German Puma earned only mid-single-digit contribution margins vs. a normal 15%-20%. As these phase out, margins structurally improve.

**KNDS / European champion**: Papperger revealed active political discussions between German and French governments: "Both governments, the German and the French government have made a clear statement that it makes sense to create a European champion for land systems." The driver was the Main Ground Combat System (MGCS), with a money decision expected May/June 2019.

**UK capacity acquisition**: Acquired 55% of BAE UK Land Systems for immediate production capacity and engineering know-how. Contrasted with Australia's greenfield MilVehCoE approach in Brisbane.

### Division Performance

**Automotive (sales EUR 2.84 billion, +4.2% FX-adjusted, margin 8.9% -- record)**:
- Q4 sales grew 7.2% despite markets turning negative in November/December
- Mechatronics: Q4 margin stayed in double digits (10%) despite R&D shift for e-mobility (~EUR 5 million impact)
- Hardparts: stable Q4 sales; margin improved to 6.4% with help from at-equity entities
- Aftermarket: 11.6% Q4 margin; efficiency measures drove 24% result improvement
- Light vehicle diesel sales declined EUR 73 million (-9.3%); share fell from 27% to 24% of total
- Non-LV business overcompensated diesel decline: +11.3% (EUR 115 million)
- China: grew 5.7% FX-adjusted in a declining market (-4%); EBIT +3.6% to EUR 79 million

**Defence (sales EUR 3.27 billion, Q4 was 39% of annual sales -- very back-end loaded)**:
- Q4 records across the board: EUR 1.3 billion quarterly sales (+24%), 14.3% margin (+300 bps)
- Weapon & Ammunition: Q4 sales +10%, margin 22.7% (from 17.9%)
- Electronic Solutions: Q4 sales +40%, result +84% -- "good business development combined with improving cost structure is clearly making inroads"
- Vehicle Systems: Q4 sales grew EUR 78 million to EUR 501 million, result +76%
- Full-year capex: EUR 101 million (slightly below midterm guidance)
- R&D impairment: EUR 24 million on AMPV (small 4x4 vehicle) -- couldn't find marketing partner

### Analyst Concerns

1. **Automotive first half weakness** (Weier, UBS): Papperger confirmed Q1 expected minus 5%, with ~6% margin at that sales level.
2. **Defence midterm margin conservatism** (Weier): Asked why only 8-9% when Puma phaseout alone is 50 bps. Papperger: "In big programs you always have risks and we are very careful, we have to deliver."
3. **Risk management on big programs** (Cohrs, Warburg): Key question about avoiding repeat of Puma/Boxer profitability issues. Papperger cited three changes: (1) better base calculations, (2) new program management and risk management teams implemented 5-6 years ago with quarterly board oversight, (3) contingencies of 2-5% negotiated into contracts.
4. **Cash flow** (Weier/Laskawi): Operating free cash flow of minus EUR 35 million was disappointing. Merch explained a EUR 100 million milestone payment arrived in the first week of January and EUR 230 million of receivables were already collected in first 10 weeks of 2019.
5. **Brexit impact** (Laskawi, Deutsche Bank): EUR 360 million UK sales (EUR 250 million Auto, EUR 110 million Defence). Papperger: "85% to 90% of our sales to UK, the customers are responsible for the duties." Risk assessed as "very small."
6. **ROCE deterioration guidance** (Cohrs): Despite higher earnings, Merch guided lower ROCE for 2019 due to growing balance sheet (higher capex, working capital) and absence of one-off positive effects.

### Forward-Looking Statements

- Defence midterm margins raised to 8-9% -- profitability convergence of Automotive and Defence expected
- "Super cycle" confirmed as underway with EUR 5.5 billion record order intake
- Electronic Solutions midterm margin target: 7-8% (from 5.6% in 2018)
- KNDS / European champion discussions "in full power" between German and French governments
- Automotive EUR 500 million e-mobility orders pipeline growing; new products for fuel cell and micro-mobility
- Defence capex to include ~EUR 50-60 million IFRS 16 impact for Australian MilVehCoE in 2020 (non-cash)

### Notable Quotes

> "2018 was an extraordinary year for Rheinmetall from our financial perspective." -- Papperger

> "As you may recall from our Capital Markets Day, we spoke of the beginning of a super cycle in the Defence business and 2018 marked the beginning of this cycle." -- Papperger

> "We are coming from a superb 2018... In the mid-term all is set for further growth and higher profitability." -- Papperger in closing

> "In big programs you always have risks and we are very careful, we have to deliver." -- Papperger on Defence margin conservatism

> "The base profit in our calculations is in good shape... We have a much better program management." -- Papperger on avoiding legacy contract margin problems

## FY2019 Results (Call: 18 March 2020)

### Key Management Statements

This call took place at the very onset of the COVID-19 pandemic and is remarkable for its dual tone: strong FY2019 results presented alongside deep uncertainty about the weeks ahead.

**Papperger**: "The group delivered an excellent set of figures in 2019. New record levels were achieved. Meaning that sales, operating result, cash generation and shareholder return have marked new highs." But he immediately qualified: "we must not forget that our two segments developed very differently."

The order backlog crossing EUR 10 billion was the standout: "Probably the most interesting KPI is our future oriented order backlog, which has crossed the 10 billion Euros threshold for the first time, and stands now at about EUR 11 billion."

On COVID, Papperger was measured but honest: "If Corona had not happened, we would have expected a slight growth of 1-3% on Group level with a margin of around 7%. We cannot guide for the Corona impact in this very dynamic and disruptive environment."

### Guidance Given

- **Pre-COVID indicative view**: Group growth 1-3%, margin ~7%
- **Actual formal guidance**: Withdrawn / suspended due to COVID uncertainty; earliest update with Q1 release
- **Automotive contribution margin**: ~35% (important for downside scenario modeling)
- **Automotive breakeven threshold**: Could manage a 10% annual sales decline and remain breakeven after realizing all cost savings (Papperger corrected to 10% from initial 15% statement)
- **Dividend proposal**: EUR 2.40 (+14%), payout ratio ~31% -- subject to AGM (May 5) potentially being postponed

### Strategic Commentary

**The two-speed company**: The contrast between Automotive and Defence had never been starker. Automotive sales dropped almost 7% with margin declining 220 bps to 6.7%, while Defence grew to EUR 3.5 billion with margin jumping to 9.8%.

**Defence export restrictions impact**: Papperger quantified the drag at EUR 90 million in 2019, "eliminating almost 3% of growth." South Africa, Saudi Arabia, and Italy were all problem areas. On Saudi trucks, Papperger outlined three scenarios: (1) April permission to deliver, (2) government compensation by law, or (3) further delay/court proceedings. On South Africa: "light at the end of the tunnel" with government agreeing to change export regulations.

**COVID crisis management** was described in unusually operational detail:
- "Corona action teams" installed immediately
- Blue-collar workforce organized into isolated groups: "If there is an infection of one group, we can carry on with work"
- 50/50 home office for white-collar workers, prioritizing parents
- China operations back to ~70% productivity with no employee infections
- Supply chains "stable for the time being"
- Key risk insight: Papperger noted they held one year of nitrocellulose stock as ammunition raw material -- "This is the reason of our risk management that we have also higher working capital"

**Automotive as an industry under siege**: "Trade wars, never ending discussions around Brexit and insecurity regarding the speed and magnitude of the powertrain transformation were dominant topics. Before Corona started to hit the headlines, discussions were centered around the de-carbonisation of our society, including city bans for diesel."

**Infantry fighting vehicle tenders**: Rheinmetall was "participating in tenders for almost EUR 10 billion for infantry fighting vehicles globally" -- a massive pipeline.

### Division Performance

**Automotive (sales -6.8%, margin 6.7%)**:
- Q4 particularly weak: sales -13%, margin declined 330 bps to 6.2%
- Mechatronics: Q4 sales -16.7% (ramp-downs, lower diesel demand, GM strike)
- Hardparts: Q4 sales -11.6% (GM strike + weak end markets)
- Aftermarket: bucked the trend with Q4 sales +7%, result +11%
- China: standout performance with +15% annual growth, Q4 +33%; new products including electric vapor pump and 5G antenna casting
- Diesel-related products suffered the single biggest decline: minus EUR 90 million
- Cash flow improved: operating free cash flow up from EUR 26 million to EUR 73 million (strict working capital and capex management)
- Self-financed R&D at 6% of sales, expected to remain at this level midterm

**Defence (sales EUR 3.5 billion, +3.5%, margin 9.8%, operating result EUR 343 million -- record)**:
- Q4 was a "fantastic performance": order intake nearly tripled to EUR 3 billion; sales +5.5%; margin 15.8%
- Weapon & Ammunition: Q4 hit by export restrictions (EUR 50 million impact from South Africa), but result still +6.5%
- Electronic Solutions: solid 5% growth; Q4 ~40% of annual sales
- Vehicle Systems: Q4 sales +20% to EUR 608 million; higher-margin tactical vehicles driving improvement
- Order backlog: EUR 10.4 billion; almost two-thirds in Vehicle Systems
- Home markets (Germany, UK, Australia) comprised 74% of sales (up from 69%)
- Key 2019 wins: UK Boxer, VJTF upgrades, 1,000 German trucks

### Analyst Concerns

1. **COVID downside scenarios** (Weier, UBS / Ubert, SocGen): The critical questions. Merch provided the 35% contribution margin for modeling. Papperger/Merch indicated they could manage up to ~10% annual sales decline in Automotive and stay breakeven. Beyond 10%: "it would be hard to cope immediately with the challenges on the cost side."
2. **Defence budget risk from fiscal crisis** (Weier): Papperger gave a crucial historical perspective: "We didn't feel a big impact on our business about this, in that crisis [Lehman, Euro crisis]. Because if you have a crisis usually defense is more important than if you have no crisis."
3. **Share price weakness** (Weier): Stock had fallen hard. Papperger said Rheinmetall was being valued "as a full auto company" despite strong Defence. He couldn't explain the discount but stressed: "At the end of the day, the performance of the company must be there and this is our job."
4. **Share buyback** (Weier): Merch firmly shut this down: "It is not today the time to think about this possible issue. We have to do very operational things."
5. **Dividend at risk from AGM postponement** (Tusa, Agency Partners): Papperger confirmed that if Berlin authorities prevented the AGM, "we cannot pay the dividend. We have to pay it later."
6. **OEM payment behavior** (Laskawi, Deutsche Bank): Merch said no signals of OEMs unwilling/unable to pay. Papperger noted OEMs were actually restocking to prepare for restart.
7. **Contract signing delays from travel restrictions** (Laskawi): Papperger acknowledged "minimum three or four weeks" delay -- "it would be unfair if we say no" -- but expected weeks not months.
8. **Vehicle Systems profitability** (Schramm, HSBC / Cohrs, Warburg): Given two-thirds of backlog in vehicles. Papperger: target is 8-9% for vehicles, Electronic Solutions 8%, Ammunition double-digit (12-14%). Service and spare parts contracts from platforms would support margins long-term.
9. **KNDS progress** (Growe, Commerzbank): "No change at the moment. We speak together, we always try to find a way."

### Forward-Looking Statements

- Pre-COVID, management expected 1-3% group growth with ~7% margin
- EUR 10 billion+ tender pipeline for infantry fighting vehicles globally
- Hungary Lynx order in final negotiation (delayed weeks, not months by COVID)
- Czech Republic vehicle program also in pipeline
- Big air defense programs globally
- Australia pressing for early delivery (no budget cut signals)
- South African export situation expected to improve in H2 2020
- Saudi Arabia decision expected April 2020 (trucks)
- Working capital build-up for ammunition raw materials as deliberate risk management

### Notable Quotes

> "If you have a crisis usually defense is more important than if you have no crisis." -- Papperger on whether COVID fiscal pressures would hit defence budgets

> "We cannot guide for the Corona impact in this very dynamic and disruptive environment." -- Papperger, suspending formal guidance

> "I have one year nitrocellulose on stock and this is the reason of our risk management that we have also higher working capital in some areas because it's cheaper to have this higher working capital, then not to be able to produce." -- Papperger revealing deep operational risk management in ammunition

> "At the end of the day, the performance of the company must be there and this is our job. Our job is to bring the performance back and to hold the company high." -- Papperger on share price frustration

> "We are seen as a full auto company." -- Papperger on market perception vs. reality

## FY2020 Results (Call: 18 March 2021)

### Key Management Statements

**Papperger** opened with empathy: "A year ago, we stood at the beginning of a crisis that affected everybody's life in a way that was completely new to all of us."

But the tone shifted quickly to cautious pride: "Although Corona was the overwhelming topic of 2020, we actually managed to do some business and in fact I would say that we have delivered a solid set of financials under these circumstances." Operating results of EUR 427 million and operating free cash flow of EUR 217 million -- both positive -- in the middle of a pandemic.

The pivotal strategic statement: "This difference in the business development accelerated our approach to initiate the transformation. The goal is to create stronger emphasis to Defence and to reduce our dependency from internal combustion engines." This was the inflection point in the Automotive-to-Defence pivot narrative.

**Merch** described the balance sheet as offering "high flexibility to develop our future businesses" and noted Moody's confirmed the investment grade rating post the February 2021 strategy update.

### Guidance Given

- **Group 2021 operational sales growth**: 7% to 9%
- **Group 2021 operating margin**: 8% to 9%
- **Cash flow to sales ratio 2021**: ~3% (midpoint of existing 2-4% range)
- **Midterm cash flow to sales target**: raised from 2-4% to 3-5%
- **Dividend payout ratio**: discussing increase from 30-35% to 35-40% midterm
- **Restructuring cost savings**: ~EUR 20 million in 2021, reaching full EUR 40 million by 2023
- **Defence service business target**: ~EUR 1 billion by 2025 (from EUR 536 million in 2020)
- **Minimum EBIT hurdle**: 10% for all divisions (explicitly stated for Sensors & Actuators)
- **Pistons business**: sale process underway; ~100 interested parties, shortlist of 20-30; target signing Q3 2021
- **New reporting structure**: five core divisions plus non-core pistons, starting Q1 2021

### Strategic Commentary

**Corporate transformation announced**: This was the call where Rheinmetall's strategic pivot crystallized. The new organizational structure featured five core divisions with pistons explicitly classified as non-core. Papperger: "The goal is to create stronger emphasis to Defence and to reduce our dependency from internal combustion engines."

**ESG commitment**: For the first time, ESG was prominently featured. A 20% share of ESG targets was introduced in long-term compensation. Rheinmetall applied to join the UN Global Compact. Carbon neutrality target set for 2035. Papperger directly addressed the ESG challenge: "Obviously, Rheinmetall is active in an industry that does not get full support from all stakeholders. But to be clear: We are convinced that Rheinmetall is contributing its share to a sustainable society that is willing and able to defend our values."

**Pistons divestiture**: Goldman Sachs appointed as advisor. ~100 interested parties, shortlisting to 20-30. Merch indicated signing by Q3 2021 (potentially stretched by COVID). This was a clean signal that the old Automotive conglomerate structure was being unwound.

**Sensor & Actuators reframing**: Papperger was emphatic that this business was NOT for sale. He saw growth from EUR 1.2 billion to EUR 2.2 billion driven by hydrogen technology: "We will show you during the next capital markets day, these new technologies." On the rest of Hardparts: "If there is someone who is paying an extraordinarily good price, we have to think about."

**M&A appetite high**: "We are looking for 30 to 50 different companies, which really fit to us. There are some targets at the moment, really near." Targets specifically on the electronic side (Sensors & Actuators) and Defence side (electronic companies, UAV companies). With over EUR 1 billion cash on the bank account, firepower was ample.

**Defence pipeline richness**: Papperger laid out order intake expectations with unusual specificity: Germany ~EUR 2 billion, UK ~EUR 1 billion, Hungary ~EUR 1 billion = EUR 4 billion base. Additional upside from Lynx programs (Slovakia ~EUR 2 billion, Australia ~EUR 5 billion). He expected "minimum one" Lynx contract beyond Hungary.

**Ammunition innovations**: New 155mm ammunition capable of 70+ km range (vs. ~40 km industry standard). Loitering ammunition entering the portfolio. Counter-drone/swarm defense systems based on 35mm airburst munition -- explicitly referencing lessons from the Azerbaijan-Armenia conflict.

**Challenger 2 LEP**: Papperger was "expecting, day by day now" the official British MoD confirmation. He highlighted the ammunition follow-on: the UK had no smoothbore gun before, so 120mm ammunition demand was incremental.

### Division Performance

**Automotive (sales -19.3% currency-adjusted, margin came back positive for the full year)**:
- Booked business collapsed 25% to EUR 2.7 billion
- Already 14% of won nomination letters were for alternative powertrains
- Q4 recovery: currency-adjusted sales +1.7% (in line with market), margin improved 100 bps to 7.2%
- Mechatronics: benefiting from cost measures implemented earlier in year
- Hardparts: weak large bore pistons; D&A relief of EUR 6 million from Q2 impairment helped
- Aftermarket: grew 5% in Q4 but hit by EUR 6 million Czech plant write-down
- China: lost a customer on pricing (Papperger: "sometimes you win, sometimes you lose"); Q4 sales -6%
- Capex aggressively managed: down from EUR 142 million to EUR 95 million (EUR 85 million cash effective)
- R&D declined ~10% but self-financed share held steady
- Fuel cell milestone: exclusive Daimler supply agreement for hydrogen recirculation blower

**Defence (sales +6% to EUR 3.7 billion, margin 11.1% -- first time double-digit)**:
- "Hardly affected" by pandemic; lockdown effects compensated during year
- Order backlog: EUR 12.9 billion (new record) + EUR 3.6 billion shadow backlog for truck/ammunition frameworks
- Hungarian Lynx: "first commercial order for this vehicle underpinning our technological and commercial edge"
- Q4 order intake +48% to EUR 4.4 billion (Hungarian Lynx + German trucks)
- Weapon & Ammunition: Q4 sales +21% to EUR 528 million, margin ~30% (historical high)
- Electronic Solutions: Q4 result +EUR 12 million despite -10% sales (favorable mix)
- Vehicle Systems: Q4 sales EUR 499 million (-20% vs. exceptional Q4 2019 project settlements)
- Home market share in order intake: ~90% (including Hungary as new home market)
- Service business grew 10% to EUR 536 million
- Won German CH-53 helicopter fleet maintenance contract
- Saudi truck license formally cancelled in December 2020; claiming damage compensation
- R&D up 24% to EUR 180 million: 130mm gun, high energy laser, AI/unmanned vehicles
- EUR 300 million impairment charges + EUR 40 million restructuring in Automotive as special items

### Analyst Concerns

1. **Auto volume guidance vs. semiconductor shortage** (Barker, Bank of America): Papperger: "The internal effect of semiconductors is, for us, very limited. We have enough semiconductors in our storage." Some OEM call-off reductions but "total impact for Rheinmetall is limited."
2. **Free cash flow conversion** (Barker / Laskawi, Deutsche Bank): Why only ~3% when EUR 150 million working capital should reverse? Merch: higher capex in both segments and milestone payment volatility. Papperger: Hungary ramp-up consuming working capital, no extraordinary items, but a big new contract with down payment could change the picture upward.
3. **Cash deployment** (Laskawi): Papperger: "The best way to use it is really to buy good companies and to create value for shareholders." Also discussed raising payout ratio to 35-40%.
4. **China customer loss** (Tusa, Agency Partners): Pure pricing issue, competitor was cheaper, Papperger wouldn't go below his price. "Sometimes you win, sometimes you lose."
5. **Saudi working capital** (Tusa): EUR 50 million tied up. Legal dispute or amicable resolution during 2021. Papperger: "We think that we lose zero euros."
6. **Defence margin sustainability** (Growe, Commerzbank): W&A at 15%+, Electronic Solutions at 10% -- were these sustainable? Papperger confirmed targets. For Vehicle Systems: ~9% realistic given internal procurement from other divisions. Pushed back on the idea of margins "easily" reaching 12%: "Nothing is wrong. But you know that we are really conservative. We want to deliver what is going into the guidance and we want to deliver 100%."
7. **German election risk** (Weier, UBS): Asked about Black/Green vs. "Ampel" coalition impact. Papperger: "As long as Germany is in the NATO, it doesn't matter which coalition is really ruling this country." Personally expected Black/Green coalition.
8. **Sensor & Actuators for sale?** (Weier): Papperger emphatically: "No." Only pistons are for sale. S&A expected to grow from EUR 1.2 billion to EUR 2.2 billion. Might consider selling other Hardparts "if there is somebody coming who is paying us unbelievable lot of money."
9. **Drone warfare impact** (Hauenstein, DZ Bank): Referencing Azerbaijan-Armenia conflict. Papperger detailed loitering ammunition plans, counter-drone systems (35mm airburst), and swarm attack defense. View: drones are additive, not substitutive -- tanks still needed, but counter-drone is a major growth area.

## Cross-Transcript Themes and Evolution

### The Defence Super Cycle Arc
- **FY2017**: Seeds planted -- "growing military spend in many countries," Land 400 as milestone
- **FY2018**: Officially declared -- "2018 marked the beginning of this cycle," EUR 8.5 billion order intake
- **FY2019**: Backlog crossed EUR 10 billion; defence margins hit 9.8%; super cycle proving out despite Automotive weakness
- **FY2020**: Defence margins broke double digits (11.1%); backlog at EUR 12.9 billion plus EUR 3.6 billion shadow; super cycle accelerating

### The Automotive Transformation Arc
- **FY2017**: Confident -- 8.7% margins, e-mobility pivot underway, 50% electrified products target by 2020
- **FY2018**: Cracks appearing -- diesel crisis, WLTP, tariffs; but margins held at 8.9% record through cost flexibility
- **FY2019**: Significant decline -- sales -7%, margins -220 bps to 6.7%; Papperger admitted Rheinmetall was "seen as a full auto company"
- **FY2020**: Crisis acceleration -- sales -19%, massive impairments; strategic decision to pivot away from ICE dependency; pistons marked for divestiture

### Margin Guidance Evolution (Defence)
- FY2017 call: guided 6-6.5% for 2018
- FY2018 call: raised midterm to 8-9%
- FY2019 call: achieved 9.8%, guided ~7% for 2020 (pre-COVID)
- FY2020 call: achieved 11.1%, guided group 8-9% for 2021, minimum 10% hurdle for all divisions

### Cash Flow Discipline
Merch consistently recommended evaluating cash flow over 2-3 year rolling periods due to milestone payment lumpiness. The strategic cash-to-sales target evolved from 2-4% to 3-5%.

### Recurring Analyst Themes
- **Automotive margin sustainability**: asked every year; management progressively lowered expectations
- **Free cash flow**: perennial concern given Defence volatility; consistently guided to look at multi-year periods
- **M&A**: asked every year; Papperger consistently said "looking" but "not under pressure"
- **Land 400 / Australia**: evolved from risk concern (FY2017) to proven execution (FY2020)
- **Export restrictions**: Saudi Arabia/South Africa drag recurring from FY2018 through FY2020
- **European consolidation / KNDS**: raised periodically; "no change" was the consistent update

### Management Style Observations
- **Papperger**: Operationally detailed, personally connected to customers and politicians, prone to giving specific deal details and pipeline numbers. Occasionally needed IR corrections (e.g., the 15% vs. 10% breakeven threshold on the FY2019 call). Unflappable optimist even at the height of COVID. His personal relationships with government officials were a recurring theme.
- **Merch**: Precise, financially conservative, consistent in messaging on cash flow, working capital, and balance sheet. Served as the technical anchor to Papperger's more expansive commentary. Repeatedly steered analysts toward multi-year evaluation frameworks.
