## PART H: STRATEGY EVOLUTION AND KEY EVENTS

### 2017 Strategy (from 2017 Annual Report, p.36)
- Two-pillar strategy: Automotive (Mobility) and Defence (Security)
- Automotive: target stable ~8.5% operating margin; China/India expansion focus
- Defence: benefiting from NATO 2% GDP target; internationalization via JV strategy
- Intrapreneur-Award innovation initiative (3D printing, e-drives, sensors)
- EUR 500m in orders for electric/hybrid vehicle components booked in 2017
- Automotive pursuing "powertrain neutrality" -- reducing dependence on combustion engines
- PESCO (Permanent Structured Cooperation) initiative seen as opportunity
- 117 locations in 33 countries; sales to customers in 146 states

### 2018 Key Events (from FY18 transcript and annual report)
- Described as beginning of Defence "super cycle"
- Australia Land 400 Phase 2 contract + truck extension: combined ~EUR 2.6bn
- Defence order intake record: EUR 5.5bn; guided EUR 4-6bn p.a. mid-term
- Return to investment grade rating (April 2018)
- Tragic incident at Somerset West (South Africa) site -- 8 fatalities
- Automotive: WLTP disruption, diesel decline, China market turning negative
- EUR 500m bond redeemed August 2017; moved to net cash position
- Sikorsky/Lockheed partnership for German heavy-lift helicopter program (~EUR 5.5bn procurement + ~EUR 2bn maintenance)
- Discussion with Raytheon, Lockheed on 10-15 potential transatlantic programs

### 2019 Key Events (from FY19/Q4 transcript and annual report)
- Automotive market deterioration: LV production -1.1% in 2018, only +0.4% forecast for 2019
- Defence MGCS (Main Ground Combat System) discussions with France progressing
- KNDS European champion concept discussed at government level
- Defence mid-term margin target: 8-9% (reflecting risk from big programs)
- Electronic Solutions restructuring bearing fruit; mid-term target 7-8% margin
- Legacy contracts (Dutch Boxer, German Puma) phasing out -- they earned only mid-single-digit margins vs normal 15-20%

### 2020: COVID and Structural Shift (from 2020 Annual Report and FY20 transcript)
- COVID-19 pandemic impact: group revenue fell to EUR 5,875m (or 5,405m on continued basis)
- EBIT collapsed to EUR 89m (full group) due to EUR 337m impairments (largely Automotive goodwill)
- Operating result EUR 426m shows underlying business remained resilient
- Cash flow from operations EUR 453m; FCF EUR 217m -- strong cash generation despite pandemic
- Beginning of "ONE Rheinmetall" strategic review

### 2021: Structural Transformation
- February 2021: "ONE Rheinmetall" announced -- dissolution of Automotive/Defence dual structure
- New 5-division structure: Vehicle Systems, Weapon and Ammunition, Electronic Solutions, Sensors and Actuators, Materials and Trade
- Jorg Grotendorst (Automotive board member) departed; board reduced from 4 to 3 members
- Pistons business classified as discontinued (IFRS 5) from May 2021
- Armin Papperger reappointed as CEO for 5 more years
- Record H1 operating result in company history
- Dividend: EUR 3.30 (up from EUR 2.00)

### 2022: Zeitenwende (from 2022 Annual Report)
- February 2022: Russian invasion of Ukraine -- "Zeitenwende" declared by German Chancellor Scholz
- German EUR 100bn Sondervermogen (special defence fund) announced
- Stock price: EUR 186.05 at year-end (up from EUR 83.06)
- Revenue EUR 6,410m; op. result EUR 754m (originally reported as 769 restated)
- FCF negative: -175m due to massive inventory build-up for capacity expansion
- Employees: 25,486 (21,788 continued)

### 2023: DAX Entry and Acceleration (from 2023 Annual Report)
- Entered DAX index
- Revenue EUR 7,176m; op. result EUR 918m; margin 12.8%
- Rheinmetall Backlog reached EUR 38,290m
- Auftragseingang: EUR 11,843m (vs 5,629m prior year) -- more than doubled
- EBIT before PPA: EUR 968m (new metric introduced)
- Balance sheet total jumped to EUR 11,707m from EUR 8,096m (M&A driven)
- Net financial debt: EUR 1,063m (from EUR 426m)

### 2024: Expansion Year (from 2025 report)
- Revenue EUR 7,715m; op. result EUR 1,389m; margin 18.0%
- ROCE: 25.7%
- Acquisition of LOC Performance (US) -- important US market milestone
- FCF: EUR 1,023m; CCR: 76.0%
- Rheinmetall Nomination: EUR 24,335m; Rheinmetall Backlog: EUR 46,943m

### 2025: Record Year and Full Defence Focus (from 2025 Annual Report)
- Revenue EUR 9,935m (+28.8%); op. result EUR 1,841m (+32.5%); margin 18.5%
- ROCE: 33.5%
- EBIT before PPA: EUR 1,850m
- FCF: EUR 1,415m (CCR: 66.2%)
- Rheinmetall Nomination: EUR 26,448m; Rheinmetall Backlog: EUR 63,761m
- Auftragsbestand: EUR 40,847m
- December 2025: Civil activities (Power Systems) classified as discontinued -- full defence focus
- Entered EURO STOXX 50 index
- Major events:
  - April 2025: Hagedorn (nitrocellulose) acquisition
  - September 2025: Largest ammunition factory in Europe opened in Unterluss
  - LRMV JV with Leonardo for Italian MBT (Panther) and IFV (Lynx)
  - 200 Puma IFVs ordered by Bundeswehr (via PSM JV)
  - MGCS Project Company founded (KNDS DE, KNDS FR, Rheinmetall, Thales)
  - F-35 fuselage mid-section production started in Weeze (with Northrop Grumman/Lockheed)
  - TaWAN LBO and IdZ-ES among largest single orders
  - Italy: Skynex air defence adopted
  - Netherlands: major mobile air defence order
  - SPOCK satellite program ordered by German customer
  - Raketenartillerie factory planned in Unterluss
- February 2026 (post balance sheet): NVL (Naval Vessels Lurssen) acquisition completed
- German defence budget 2025: EUR 86.4bn (EP14: EUR 62.3bn + Sondervermogen EUR 24.1bn)
- German constitutional amendment March 2025: new debt allowance for defence/security
