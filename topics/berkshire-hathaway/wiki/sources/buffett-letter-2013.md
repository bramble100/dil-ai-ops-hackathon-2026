## Summary

The 2013 letter reports an 18.2% gain in per-share book value ($134,973; 49-year CAGR 19.7%) against the S&P's 32.4% — one of ten underperformance years, all but one when the S&P gained >15%. The letter's highlight is a landmark essay on investing fundamentals using farm and real estate parables to teach six core principles. On the operating front, two major acquisitions (Heinz with 3G Capital for ~$12.3B, NV Energy for $5.6B by MidAmerican), Marmon and ISCAR reach 100% ownership ($3.5B combined), and the Powerhouse Five deliver record $10.8B pre-tax. Insurance extends to 11 consecutive profitable years ($22B cumulative; float $77.2B) with BHSI launched as a commercial insurance entrant. Buffett reveals his 90/10 will instruction (Vanguard S&P 500 index fund) and pays tribute to Ben Graham.

## Key Claims

- Book value +18.2% to $134,973/share; 49-year CAGR 19.7%; overall gain 693,518%
- Two-pillar intrinsic value update: per-share investments $129,253 (+13.6%); per-share pre-tax operating earnings $9,116 (+12.8%); since 1970, investments 19.3% CAGR and earnings 20.6% CAGR
- Heinz acquired with 3G Capital: Berkshire paid $8B for 9% preferred (estimated ~12% annual return) plus $4.25B for half the common; 3G operates, Berkshire finances — a "partnership template" for future deals
- NV Energy acquired by MidAmerican for $5.6B; serves 88% of Nevada's population; renewable energy expansion expected
- Marmon reaches 100% ownership and ISCAR reaches 100% (Wertheimer put exercised) for combined $3.5B; adds $300M pre-tax and $800M cash; $1.8B "nonsensical" accounting charge against book value
- Powerhouse Five (BNSF, MidAmerican, ISCAR, Lubrizol, Marmon) earn record $10.8B pre-tax, up $758M from 2012; only MidAmerican was owned nine years ago
- Insurance: 11 consecutive profitable years; $22B cumulative pre-tax; float $77.2B (BH Reinsurance $37.2B, General Re $20.0B, GEICO $12.6B, Other $7.4B)
- BHSI (Berkshire Hathaway Specialty Insurance) formed June 2013; Peter Eastwood leads; commercial insurance targeting Fortune 500; instant market acceptance due to Berkshire's financial strength
- Ajit Jain: $37B float from a standing start in 1985; "a feat no other insurance CEO has come close to matching"
- General Re: "now a gem"; Tad Montross; four insurance commandments restated; international life reinsurance growth
- GEICO: #2 US auto insurer (passed Allstate); Tony Nicely 52 years; cost advantage is an "enduring moat"; economic goodwill estimated approaching $20B (vs. $15.5B carried on books)
- Big Four investments: AmEx 14.2%, Coca-Cola 9.1%, IBM 6.3%, Wells Fargo 9.2%; combined $4.4B Berkshire's share of 2013 earnings (only $1.4B reported as dividends); $39B unrealized gains; each 0.1% share increase = $50M annual earnings
- Bank of America warrants: 700M shares at $5B before Sept 2021; worth $10.9B at yearend — effectively fifth-largest equity investment
- Energy Future Holdings: sold for $259M; total pre-tax loss $873M (after $837M interest received); "Next time I'll call Charlie"
- 25 bolt-on acquisitions contracted for $3.1B aggregate
- Record $11B capex on plant and equipment; 89% in US; roughly 2x depreciation
- Todd Combs and Ted Weschler each running >$7B; outperformed both S&P and Buffett
- "Some Thoughts About Investing" essay: 1986 Nebraska farm ($280K, ~10% normalized return, now worth 5x+) and 1993 NYU retail property (~10% yield, distributions now >35% of original equity); six principles derived
- 90/10 will instruction: 10% short-term government bonds, 90% very low-cost S&P 500 index fund (suggests Vanguard)
- Ben Graham tribute: The Intelligent Investor (1949) changed Buffett's financial life; GEICO was Graham's unnamed investment bonanza; Northern Pacific recommendation → now part of BNSF
- Non-real amortization: $648M charges in MSR segment; only ~20% "real"; EBITDA critique: "button your wallet"
- MSR segment: $95.3B revenue; $6.7B pre-tax; 16.7% after-tax ROE on $25B net tangible assets
- NFM Dallas: 433-acre site, 1.8M sqft under one roof, opening 2014; Mrs. B's 1946 financials ($72,264 net worth, $50 cash)
- Clayton: 29,547 homes (4.7% US single-family); 326,569 mortgages/$13.6B; $416M pre-tax (up from $255M)
- BNSF: $22B revenue; $3.8B net earnings; $4B capex (record, 2x depreciation); 15% US inter-city ton-miles; 9:1 interest coverage
- MidAmerican: $2.1B operating earnings; 7% US wind generation; renewables portfolio to cost $15B; #1 customer satisfaction (95.3%); HomeServices 22,114 agents; NV Energy broadens earnings base
- Pension crisis warning: public entities' unfunded promises are a "gigantic financial tapeworm"; 1975 memo to Katharine Graham reproduced
- 330,745 total employees (including Heinz); headquarters: 25 people, one floor

## Relevance

This letter contains one of Buffett's most important standalone investing essays — the farm/NYU parables distill his philosophy into six actionable principles accessible to any investor. The 90/10 will instruction and Ben Graham tribute cement the index-fund message for non-professional investors. On the business front, the Heinz/3G partnership template introduces a new acquisition structure, the Powerhouse Five demonstrate the capital-intensive pivot's earnings power, and BHSI expands Berkshire's commercial insurance footprint. The Marmon/ISCAR completions cap multi-year staged acquisitions.
