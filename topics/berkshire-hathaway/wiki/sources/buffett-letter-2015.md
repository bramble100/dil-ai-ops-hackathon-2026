## Summary

The 2015 letter (Berkshire's 51st year under Buffett) centers on Precision Castparts Corp — a $32B all-cash acquisition making PCC the sixth "Powerhouse" member — alongside a long productivity essay tracing efficiency gains from farming to railroads, auto insurance, and electric utilities. Book value rose 6.4% to $155,501, with Buffett emphasizing the growing gap between intrinsic value and book value. Float reached $87.7B (13 consecutive profitable years, $26.2B cumulative). GEICO hit 11.4% market share with a detailed founding history tracing the direct-marketing model from USAA through the Goodwins. The Kraft Heinz merger closed, and Buffett contrasts 3G's efficiency model with Berkshire's decentralized, culture-preserving approach. A climate change section invokes "Noah's Law" (Pascal's Wager analogy) while arguing insurance is protected by annual repricing.

## Key Claims

- Book value +6.4% to $155,501/share; 51-year CAGR 19.2%; overall gain 798,981%
- PCC acquired for $32B+ cash — world's premier aerospace components supplier; 30,466 employees, 162 plants, 13 countries; Mark Donegan CEO; Todd Combs identified the opportunity
- Powerhouse Five earned $13.1B (up $650M); Powerhouse Six announced for 2016 with PCC
- BNSF record $6.8B pre-tax, $5.8B capex (record for any US railroad), 17% of US intercity freight; Matt Rose and Carl Ice
- BHE: 7% US wind, 6% solar; Iowa 47% wind; 6.8¢/KWH vs. 10.4¢ national; Paris Climate commitments; no rate increase for 16 years
- Float $87,722M (13 consecutive profitable years); $26.2B cumulative underwriting profit; $1.8B in 2015
- GEICO: 11.4% market share (from 2.5% in 1995); 14.7% expense ratio; $22.6B premium; Tony Nicely 54 years; 14M policyholders; 34,000 employees; detailed founding history (Goodwins, USAA, 1936)
- General Re: Tad Montross; four commandments restated; international life reinsurance; "now a gem"
- Ajit Jain: $250B mega-cat resilience restated; joined 1986 Saturday with no insurance experience
- BHSI: Peter Eastwood; $1B annual premium in under three years
- Kraft Heinz merger: 325.4M shares (~27%); $9.8B cost; $27B sales; $720M preferred annual payout; 3G/Berkshire partnership model contrasted
- Big Four: AmEx 15.6%, Coke 9.3%, IBM 8.4%, Wells 9.8%; $4.7B look-through; Hope Diamond restated
- Bank of America warrants: 700M shares at $5B; worth $11.8B; "effectively fourth largest"
- Clayton: 34,397 homes (45% US manufactured); $12.8B mortgage portfolio; 100% risk retention; $59,942 avg loan; $157M foreclosure costs on 8,444 loans; regulatory scrutiny (65 exams, $38,200 fines)
- Marmon: 133,220 railcars (25,085 purchased from GE); 97% leased; chemicals/refined petroleum lead items
- 29 bolt-on acquisitions ($634M aggregate); 10¼ Fortune 500 equivalents
- MSR segment: $107.8B revenue; 18.4% after-tax on $25.6B net tangible assets
- Non-real amortization: $1.1B total; ~20% real; EBITDA critique expanded ("watch their noses lengthen"); railroad depreciation understates true capital needs
- Intrinsic value framework: insurance underwriting now included in Pillar 2 for the first time; two-pillar update: $159,794/share investments (+8.3%), $12,304/share earnings (+2.1%)
- Productivity essay: farming 11M→3M workers; railroads 1.35M→187K; BNSF 1996-2015 ton-miles +71%/employees +4%; GEICO cost advantage history; BHE Iowa 16 years no rate increase
- Six-point blueprint for successors: (1) improve subsidiary earnings, (2) bolt-ons, (3) investee growth, (4) repurchase at discount, (5) occasional large acquisitions, (6) rarely issue shares
- Climate change: "Noah's Law" (Pascal's Wager); annual repricing protects insurers; super-cat rates fallen
- Important risks: BNSF coal volume decline, driverless cars threat to GEICO, print circulation, online retail, cyber/bio/nuclear/chemical attack as "one clear, present and enduring danger"
- America optimism: GDP per capita $56,000 (6x 1930); 2% real growth = $19,000/capita gain per generation; "babies born today are the luckiest crop in history"
- First annual meeting webcast (Yahoo!); 40,000+ attendance; NFM $44.2M Berkshire weekend
- Home office: 25 people (no change); 361,270 employees worldwide

## Relevance

The 2015 letter is a transitional year: PCC marks the shift from "Powerhouse Five" to "Powerhouse Six" and represents the largest-ever all-cash acquisition. The productivity essay is the most extensive social commentary since the "Productivity and Prosperity" themes of prior years, using Berkshire subsidiaries as case studies for American economic dynamism. The climate change section (Noah's Law/Pascal's Wager) is Buffett's most nuanced treatment of the topic. Insurance underwriting is now formally included in Pillar 2 of intrinsic value, reflecting the stabilization of that earnings stream. The Kraft Heinz discussion crystallizes the 3G partnership model and its philosophical contrast with Berkshire's approach. The six-point successor blueprint codifies the operating model.
