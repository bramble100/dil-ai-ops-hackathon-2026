## Summary

The 2019 letter centers on the power of retained earnings — tracing the idea from Edgar Lawrence Smith's 1924 book through Keynes to Berkshire's own practice. GAAP net income swung from $4B (2018) to $81.4B (2019), a "crazy 1,900% increase" driven entirely by unrealized equity gains, while operating earnings held steady at $24B. Buffett delivers an extended essay on board governance, critiquing director independence theater and acquisition advisors. Float reaches $129.4B. GUARD Insurance's spectacular growth is highlighted. BHE celebrates its 20th year under Berkshire, approaching wind self-sufficiency in Iowa.

## Key Claims

- GAAP: $81.4B ($24B operating + $3.7B realized + $53.7B unrealized) — "a crazy 1,900% increase" over 2018's $4B; "Focus on operating earnings"
- **Retained earnings power** essay: Edgar Lawrence Smith's 1924 book; Keynes review ("element of compound interest"); Carnegie/Rockefeller/Ford reinvested; before Smith, stocks were "speculations" and "Gentlemen preferred bonds"
- 10 largest holdings: dividends $3.8B vs retained earnings $8.3B — the invisible engine of Berkshire's compounding
- Acquisitions likened to marriage: "They start with a joyful wedding — but then reality tends to diverge from pre-nuptial expectations"; many have caused Buffett to "wonder what I was thinking when I proposed"
- Non-insurance: BNSF + BHE $8.3B (+6%); total net $17.7B (+3%); five tiers of non-insurance businesses
- Lubrizol Rouen fire: Berkshire insured its own subsidiary — "Let not the left hand know what the right hand doeth" (Matthew 6:3)
- Float $129,423M; 16 of last 17 profitable years; $27.5B cumulative; $400M in 2019
- **GUARD Insurance** (Wilkes-Barre, PA): purchased 2012 for $221M; premium grew 379% to $1.9B by 2019; float +265%; Sy Foguel CEO — "Bingo and bingo"
- **BHE 20th anniversary:** Iowa wind will reach self-sufficiency by 2021 (25.2M MWh produced vs 24.6M consumed); rates <1%/year increase since 2000; competitor 61% higher (to widen to 70%); $28B retained earnings (never paid a dividend); ready for $100B+ utility projects
- Board governance essay: director compensation creates subconscious conflicts ($250-300K = 3-4x median household income); "cocker spaniel, not pit bull" selection; NWD independence is theater; "Bobby Fischer must play only chess"; acquisition advisors should be pro AND con (10:1 payout); Berkshire's 62-year board experience across 21 companies
- **$5B in share repurchases** (~1% of company); will become more aggressive if discount widens
- Succession: "Charlie and I long ago entered the urgent zone"; five factors for optimism; Buffett's 99% of net worth in BRK; will directs no sales; 12-15 year distribution period
- Berkshire paid $3.6B federal tax (1.5% of all US corporate income tax)
- Ajit Jain and Greg Abel to get more annual meeting exposure — "They are outstanding individuals"
- Three acquisition criteria: (1) good returns on net tangible capital, (2) able and honest managers, (3) sensible price

## Relevance

The 2019 letter's retained-earnings essay provides the theoretical foundation for Berkshire's entire strategy — connecting Adam Smith-era compounding to modern-day Berkshire. The board governance critique is Buffett's most detailed in the letters, addressing the structural conflicts that prevent directors from challenging management. The BHE 20-year retrospective documents the most ambitious clean-energy transformation by any US utility. The GUARD Insurance discovery illustrates that small, well-managed acquisitions continue alongside the "elephant-sized" deal-seeking.
