# Buffett's 2016 Shareholder Letter

## Summary

The 2016 letter ($27.5B net worth gain, +10.7%, book value $172,108) opens with an extended meditation on the growing gap between book value and intrinsic value — driven by GAAP's asymmetric accounting for winners vs. losers. The intellectual centerpieces are the share repurchase philosophy essay (price-dependence, the 120%-of-book threshold, and the three-partner analogy) and the near-final report on "The Bet" (S&P 500 index fund 85.4% cumulative vs. five funds-of-funds averaging 2.2% over nine years). Buffett delivers his most passionate tribute to Jack Bogle and the strongest condemnation of the fee-extraction machinery of Wall Street. On the operating side, float surges past $91B with 14 consecutive years of underwriting profit ($28B cumulative), GEICO reaches 12% market share, and Tad Montross retires from General Re after 39 years. A lengthy "adjusted earnings" critique demolishes restructuring-charge and stock-option exclusions.

## Key Claims

- Book value $172,108/share (+10.7%); 52-year CAGR 19.0%; overall gain 884,319%
- Intrinsic value now "far exceeds" book value; $15.5B insurance goodwill was already on books in 2000 when float was $28B — float has since grown $64B with no corresponding book value increase
- Float $91,577M; recently wrote a policy pushing float past $100B; 14 consecutive years of underwriting profit totaling $28B pre-tax
- GEICO: ~12% market share (up from 2.5% in 1995); 36,085 employees; growth accelerated H2 2016 as competitors retreated from pricing pressure; "when insurance prices increase, people shop more — and when they shop, GEICO wins"
- General Re: Tad Montross retired after 39 years; Kara Raiguel (16 years with Ajit) named CEO
- BHSI: $1.3B volume (+40%); Peter Eastwood; "destined to become one of the world's leading P/C insurers"
- Share repurchase essay: three-partner analogy (buyout at $900 vs. $1,100); repurchases only sensible below intrinsic value; CEOs should declare "what is smart at one price is stupid at another"; Berkshire threshold: 120% of book value
- "The Bet" nine-year results: Vanguard S&P 500 index fund +85.4% cumulative; five funds-of-funds averaged +2.2%; ~60% of all gains diverted to two layers of management fees
- Jack Bogle tribute: "If a statue is ever erected to honor the person who has done the most for American investors, the hands-down choice should be Jack Bogle"
- "Adjusted earnings" critique: restructuring charges recur every year ("Berkshire has been restructuring from the first day we took over in 1965"); stock-based compensation must be expensed ("If CEOs want to leave out stock-based compensation... they should affirm one of two propositions")
- BHE: Iowa wind 55% of retail MWH (89% by 2020); rates 7.1¢/KWH vs. national 10.3¢; promised no rate increase until 2029; "Iowa is the Saudi Arabia of America" for wind
- BNSF: 3¢/ton-mile vs. 4-5¢ competitors; four times more fuel-efficient than trucks; $8.9B combined BHE+BNSF capex; EBITDA/interest "seriously flawed" — use earnings/interest
- Manufacturing/Service/Retailing: 44 businesses reporting to HQ; $120B revenue; 24% after-tax ROE on $24B tangible assets; $15.4B intangibles to amortize (~20% real)
- Clayton Homes: America's largest home builder (42,075 units, 5% all new homes); branched into site-built (2015); $587 average monthly payment; 93% success rate on loss-mitigation programs
- Marmon railcar: fleet utilization 91% (down from 97%); GE fleet purchase impact; earnings to decline in 2017
- Dexter Shoe revised cost: 25,203 shares worth $6B+ at 2016 yearend
- Investments: Apple enters at $6.7B cost/$7.1B market; BofA $5B preferred + 700M share warrant ($10.5B unrealized); IBM $13.8B cost/$13.5B market
- America optimism: national wealth $90T from 240-year standing start; "miraculous"
- Berkshire stock not held "forever" — Economic Principle 11 covers controlled businesses, not marketable securities; sentence added to clarify

## Relevance

This letter deepens the fee/index-fund thesis with nine years of hard data from The Bet, delivers the definitive share repurchase framework, and marks operational milestones (float >$91B, GEICO 12%, Tad Montross succession). The "adjusted earnings" essay extends the accounting integrity thread across decades.
