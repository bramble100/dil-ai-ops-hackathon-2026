## Summary

Greg Abel's first annual letter as CEO — a historic transition after 60 years of Buffett's authorship. Abel formally articulates Berkshire's culture and six foundational values for the first time: Decentralized Model, Integrity, Financial Strength, Capital Discipline, Risk Management, and Operational Excellence. Operating earnings of $44.5B (below 2024's $47.4B record, above $37.5B five-year average), with $46B net operating cash flows. Float reaches $176B. Two acquisitions announced: OxyChem (industrial chemicals from Occidental) and Bell Laboratories (rodent control). BNSF margin improving but "gap to industry's best remains too wide." PCC aerospace recovery generates $2.4B operating cash. Equity portfolio: $194B concentrated in nine holdings yielding 10% on $24.5B cost. Kraft Heinz called "disappointing." Marc Hamburg retiring; Chuck Chang successor. Adam Johnson named president of consumer/service/retailing (32 companies). The letter's recurring theme: stewardship over founding genius.

## Key Claims

- Greg Abel became CEO; Warren Buffett remains Chairman, "in the office five days a week"; Buffett's shares will "all go to philanthropy over the 10 years or so following his passing"
- Operating earnings: $44.5B (2025) vs. $47.4B (2024) vs. $37.5B five-year average
- Net cash flows from operations: $46B (vs. >$40B five-year average)
- Six foundational values formally articulated: Decentralized Model, Integrity, Financial Strength, Capital Discipline, Risk Management, Operational Excellence
- Combined ratio 87.1% across P/C businesses (5yr avg 90.7%, 10yr avg 93.0%, 20yr avg 92.2%)
- Float: $176B (from $171B in 2024, $88B in 2015); insurance businesses returned $29B to Berkshire in 2025 (up to $31B permitted)
- GEICO: improved cost structure, stronger underwriting, better risk segmentation; rate increases restored margins but lowered retention; competitors' cuts may extend pressure into 2026
- Reinsurance/primary groups facing pricing headwinds as new capital enters market; "we expect to write less reinsurance premium"
- Four structural insurance advantages: significant capital, manager autonomy (no quarterly targets), underwriting discipline, long-term focus
- BNSF: $8.1B net operating cash, $4.4B dividends to Berkshire; operating margin improved to 34.5% from 32.0%; "gap to industry's best remains too wide"; each 1pp margin = ~$230M; potential Union Pacific–Norfolk Southern merger (Berkshire "not interested" in acquiring another Class I)
- BHE: $8.4B net operating cash; 24% below national retail electric rate; AI/data center demand (paid for by those customers); wildfire mitigation leadership; PacifiCorp settlements for 2020 Labor Day fires; "not an insurer of last resort"
- PCC: $2.4B net operating cash (vs. $0.9B avg in 2021-22, $1.7B in 2015); Jenkintown fire response showcased operational excellence; aerospace recovery
- OxyChem acquisition: chlorine/caustic soda for construction/industrial; "compelling addition" from Occidental
- Bell Laboratories acquisition: rodent control; Steve Levy CEO; Malcolm Stack founder; "high margins, good growth, easy to understand, always needed" — "we only wish it had been ten times bigger"
- Shaw: "some difficulties were self-inflicted" in hard-surface transition; rebuilding manufacturing and operational discipline
- Clayton: resilient business model; efficient manufacturing + integrated financing; affordable housing nationwide
- NetJets: ~1,100 aircraft in 150+ countries; Adam Johnson president (10 years as NetJets CEO); transformed from "challenged business model into a successful enterprise"
- Pilot: Pro Preference 27%→35% (2022-2025); $1.7B net operating cash; "we should be #1"; modernizing facilities, expanding EV charging; Berkshire first invested 2017 but management delayed until 2023 — "that mistake will not happen again"
- Equity portfolio top four: Apple $62.0B (1.6%), AmEx $56.1B (22.1%), Coke $28.0B (9.3%), Moody's $12.6B (13.9%) — $158.6B on $9.1B cost; dividends $1.7B
- Japanese five: Mitsubishi $9.2B, ITOCHU $8.9B, Mitsui $8.8B, Marubeni $4.5B, Sumitomo $4.0B — $35.4B on $15.4B cost; dividends $862M; yen borrowing at avg 1.2%, 5.75yr WAL
- Combined top-nine holdings: $194B market value (2/3 of $297.8B equity portfolio), $2.5B dividends, 10% yield on $24.5B cost
- Kraft Heinz: "disappointing... well short of adequate return" even with preferred equity
- Ted Weschler manages ~6% of investments; Todd Combs no longer managing investments (running GEICO)
- Cash and Treasuries exceed $370B
- Marc Hamburg (CFO) retiring June 2027; Chuck Chang successor; Mike O'Sullivan hired as first General Counsel
- Rebecca Liebert (Lubrizol CEO) assumes OxyChem responsibility alongside Lubrizol
- BRK +10.9% in 2025 vs. S&P +17.9%; 19.7% compounded annual gain (1965-2025); 6,099,294% overall gain
- BNSF Swinomish Indian Tribal Community resolution: acknowledged past mistakes, apologized, built partnership

## Relevance

This letter marks the most significant leadership transition in Berkshire's history. Abel's formal articulation of six foundational values creates a constitutional document for post-Buffett Berkshire — moving from personality-driven wisdom to codified operating principles. The letter is notably more corporate in tone than Buffett's folksy style, with structured sections on each business segment and explicit performance metrics. The candid assessments of BNSF's margin gap, Shaw's self-inflicted problems, Pilot's delayed management, and Kraft Heinz's disappointing returns demonstrate that Abel will maintain Berkshire's tradition of honest reporting. The $370B+ cash/Treasury position and $176B float signal unprecedented scale.
