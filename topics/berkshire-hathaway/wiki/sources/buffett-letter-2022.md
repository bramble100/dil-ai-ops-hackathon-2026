## Summary

The 2022 letter is built around the "Secret Sauce" of patient ownership: Coca-Cola and American Express, each acquired for $1.3B in the mid-1990s, now worth $25B and $22B respectively, delivering $704M and $302M in annual dividends. Buffett reveals that Berkshire's satisfactory 58-year results came from "about a dozen truly good decisions — about one every five years." Alleghany Corporation's acquisition adds to float ($147B→$164B, 8,000-fold since 1967). Operating earnings hit a record $30.8B while GAAP earnings gyrated wildly ($5.5B to −$43.8B by quarter), reinforcing the "worse-than-useless" GAAP verdict. Charlie Munger's wisdom is featured in a long collection of aphorisms. Berkshire is the largest owner of eight S&P 500 giants.

## Key Claims

- Coke: $1.3B cost (1994) → $25B market value; dividends from $75M (1994) to $704M (2022); "the weeds wither away in significance as the flowers bloom"
- AmEx: $1.3B cost (1995) → $22B market value; dividends from $41M to $302M
- "About a dozen truly good decisions — about one every five years" explains Berkshire's 58-year success
- Operating earnings record $30.8B; GAAP quarterly earnings ranged +$18.2B to −$43.8B — "100% misleading"
- Alleghany Corporation acquired (Joe Brandon); float rises from $147B to $164B; 8,000-fold increase since 1967
- Berkshire is largest owner of eight S&P 500 companies: AmEx, BofA, Chevron, Coke, HP, Moody's, OXY, Paramount
- BNSF: $5.9B earnings; BHE: $4.3B earnings — each would be an S&P 500 member independently
- Schumpeter's "creative destruction" invoked: capitalism creates losers alongside improved goods/services
- Federal taxes: $32B over the decade (0.1% of all Treasury receipts); physical stack metaphor (21 miles of $100 bills)
- "Bold imaginative accounting" = "one of the shames of capitalism"; earnings manipulation "disgusting"
- Munger wisdom collection: 14+ aphorisms on patience, rationality, leverage danger, dead people, railroads
- "Find a very smart high-grade partner — preferably slightly older than you — and then listen very carefully"
- Market value: +4.0% (S&P −18.1%); CAGR 19.8%; overall gain 3,787,464%
- American Tailwind continued: "I have yet to see a time when it made sense to make a long-term bet against America"

## Relevance

The "Secret Sauce" section is the single best illustration of patience-based compounding in all 49 letters. Comparing a $1.3B bond investment (static $80M income, shrinking to 0.3% of net worth) against Coke/Amex (growing dividends, $47B combined value, ~10% of net worth) demonstrates the flowers-and-weeds principle in concrete dollar terms. The "one good decision every five years" confession humanizes the myth of genius investing. The Alleghany acquisition quietly extends the float franchise. Munger's collected wisdom — likely his last major appearance in the letters — reads as valedictory.
