## Summary

The 2011 letter reports a modest 4.6% gain in book value ($99,860/share, 47-year CAGR 19.8%) but is intellectually one of the richest, centered on three major essays: the share repurchase framework (with IBM as a worked example), the "Basic Choices for Investors" three-category taxonomy (currency-based, nonproductive, productive assets), and the continued housing depression analysis. Lubrizol ($4.5B specialty chemicals) is the year's headline acquisition, while the Big Four equity portfolio — now American Express, Coca-Cola, IBM, and Wells Fargo — replaces Gillette with IBM as a permanent pillar. Todd Combs and Ted Weschler join as investment successors. Float reaches a record $70.6B with nine consecutive years of underwriting profit totaling $17B.

## Key Claims

- Book value $99,860/share (+4.6%); 47-year CAGR 19.8%; overall gain 513,055%
- Lubrizol acquired September 16; pre-tax profits grew $147M→$1,085M under James Hambrick (since 2004); three bolt-on acquisitions ($493M) agreed immediately
- "Fabulous Five" (BNSF, Iscar, Lubrizol, Marmon, MidAmerican) earned >$9B pre-tax; only MidAmerican was owned seven years earlier ($393M pre-tax)
- Eight Berkshire subsidiaries now qualify for the Fortune 500 individually
- Record $8.2B capex (~95% in the US), smashing previous record by $2B+
- Float $70.6B (up from $65.8B); nine consecutive profitable underwriting years totaling ~$17B
- GEICO market share 9.3% (from 2.0% when Nicely became CEO 18 years earlier); $15.4B premiums; Tony Nicely completed 50 years of service
- Ajit Jain: $34B float from a standing start in 1985; "a feat that no CEO of any other insurer has come close to matching"
- General Re: Tad Montross observes all four insurance commandments; "now it's a treasure"
- IBM: $10.9B investment (63.9M shares, 5.5%); Buffett explains why shareholders should root for lower stock prices during repurchase programs
- Bank of America: $5B 6% preferred + warrants for 700M shares at $7.14 (expire Sept 2, 2021)
- Big Four (AmEx 13.0%, Coke 8.8%, IBM 5.5%, Wells Fargo 7.6%): $862M dividends but $3.3B share of earnings; $2.4B unreported earnings create "at least that amount of value"
- Share repurchase framework: two conditions (ample liquidity + material discount to intrinsic value); 110% of book value limit; $20B minimum cash; bought $67M before price advanced
- Three investment categories essay: (1) currency-based (dangerous — $1 in 1965 = $7 today), (2) nonproductive (gold cube vs. pile B: all US cropland + 16 Exxon Mobils + $1T walking-around money), (3) productive assets (businesses, farms, real estate) — "the runaway winner... and by far the safest"
- Energy Future Holdings: ~$2B bonds, gas-price bet gone wrong; $1B writedown in 2010, $390M in 2011; "a major unforced error"
- Swiss Re, Goldman, GE preferreds redeemed at $12.8B aggregate — ~$1.2B in annual pre-tax income lost
- Housing recovery prediction "dead wrong"; five housing-related companies earned $513M vs. $1.8B in 2006; 43,315 employees vs. 58,769; "hormones take over"
- See's record $83M pre-tax; $1.65B cumulative since purchase ($25M cost); Brad Kinstler CEO since 2006
- NFM record earnings (10x 1983 level); 433-acre tract acquired north of Dallas for new store — "almost certain to be the highest-volume home-furnishings store in the country"
- BNSF: $19.5B revenue, $4.74B pre-tax earnings; moves ~15% of all US inter-city ton-miles; $1.8B excess capex over depreciation
- MidAmerican: $1.98B operating earnings; 3,316 MW wind by end 2012; two solar projects ~$3B; Greg Abel
- Marmon: to 80%; $614M GAAP write-off with no economic reality; 10 of 11 sectors gained earnings
- Clayton: 7% of homes built nationally; manufactured housing depression continues; old-fashioned lending validates sensible underwriting
- NetJets: Jordan Hansell takes over April; $227M pre-tax; China expansion planned
- Derivatives: insurance-like contracts winding down ($3.4B premiums vs. $2.6B payments); equity puts $8.5B liability, $6.2B settlement value
- Todd Combs: $1.75B portfolio (at cost); Ted Weschler: "will soon create one of similar size"; 80%/20% compensation split with partner
- Succession: one CEO candidate + two backups; three CIO candidates; "when a transfer of responsibility is required, it will be seamless"
- 23-person Omaha headquarters ("all on one floor"); 17,839-page federal income tax return

## Relevance

The three-categories essay is Buffett's most comprehensive statement on inflation, asset classes, and long-term investing — superseding the 1981 tapeworm essay as the definitive treatment of why productive assets outperform. The IBM investment and repurchase-math essay extend the Mr. Market / owner-earnings framework into the era of massive corporate buybacks. The share repurchase policy (110% of book) formalizes a tool Buffett had discussed theoretically for decades. The Fabulous Five structure ($9B+ from five subsidiaries) demonstrates the capital-intensive pivot first signaled with BNSF.
