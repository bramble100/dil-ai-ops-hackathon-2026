## Overview

TTI is a Fort Worth-based distributor of electronic components, acquired by Berkshire in late 2006. Founder Paul Andrews Jr. built the company over 35 years from $112,000 in sales to $1.3 billion. The deal — introduced by John Roach (Chairman of Justin Industries) — was made in a single morning meeting.

## Key Facts

- **Acquired:** Late 2006
- **Founder/Owner:** Paul Andrews Jr. (~80% owner, age 64)
- **Revenue:** $1.3B
- **Business:** Electronic components distribution
- **Headquarters:** Fort Worth, Texas
- **Referral:** John Roach (former Chairman of Justin Industries, a Berkshire subsidiary)
- **Deal speed:** Meeting November 15, morning; deal before lunch

## Significance

TTI exemplifies Berkshire's "buyer of choice" thesis for founder-led businesses. Paul Andrews rejected three alternatives:

1. **Strategic buyer** — would pursue "synergies" by dismantling what he built, uprooting employees
2. **Private equity** — would load the company with debt and flip it
3. **Auction after death** — trust officer/lawyer-run process would be disruptive and destructive

Berkshire offered the fourth option: permanent ownership with the founder continuing to run the business he loved. Andrews later wrote: "After our meeting, I am confident that Berkshire is the right owner for TTI... I am proud of our past and excited about our future"[^1].
