## Overview

Forest River is the #2 recreational vehicle manufacturer in the US, acquired by Berkshire Hathaway on August 31, 2005. The deal originated from a **two-page fax** sent by founder Pete Liegl after reading Berkshire's acquisition criteria in the 2003 annual report — a textbook example of the four-filters acquisition process.

## Key Facts

- **Acquired:** August 31, 2005
- **Revenue:** $1.6B
- **Plants:** 60
- **Employees:** 5,400
- **Founder/CEO:** Pete Liegl
- **Origin story:** Liegl bought Forest River from an LBO operator in 1996 for a "token amount" when the company was near bankruptcy. Built it into the second-largest RV maker in the US through operational excellence and disciplined growth.
- **Deal process:** Liegl's two-page fax → Buffett's quick evaluation → deal closed. "It took me only a few minutes to realize that Pete was a straight-shooter."

## Significance

Forest River exemplifies the Berkshire acquisition model at its most efficient:

- **Filter (1):** RV manufacturing is within Buffett's circle of competence — straightforward manufacturing business
- **Filter (2):** #2 industry position with 60-plant scale advantage
- **Filter (3):** Pete Liegl's character evident from his entrepreneurial story — bought a near-bankrupt company, built it with his own talent, sought a permanent home rather than another financial buyer
- **Filter (4):** Price acceptable; all-cash deal

Liegl's quip captures why owner-operators choose Berkshire: "It was easier to sell my business than renew my driver's license." The deal also illustrates Berkshire's unique sourcing advantage — Buffett's annual reports serve as permanent "help wanted" ads that attract quality sellers[^1].
