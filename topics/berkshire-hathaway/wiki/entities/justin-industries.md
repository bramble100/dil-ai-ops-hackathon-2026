## Overview

A Texas-based conglomerate combining the leading Western boot brands (Justin, Tony Lama, Nocona, Chippewa) and Acme Brick — the leading brick producer in Texas and five neighboring states (11.7% of national brick output). Acquired by Berkshire for $570M cash in July 2000.

## Key Facts

- **Acquired:** July 2000
- **Price:** $570M cash
- **Founder/major shareholder:** John Justin (died late February 2001)
- **Chairman:** John Roach
- **Two divisions:** Acme Brick (Harrold Melton CEO); Justin Boot (Randy Watson CEO)
- **Acme Brick:** >1 billion bricks/year, 22 plants; 75% brand recognition in Texas ("When Texans are asked to name a brand of brick, 75% respond Acme")

## Acquisition Story

On May 4, 2000, Buffett received a fax from Mark Jones (an airplane broker who had sold Berkshire a jet in 1986) proposing Berkshire join a group to acquire an unnamed company. Buffett replied that he doesn't invest with others, but would pay a commission for a deal he later completes. Jones revealed it was Justin Industries. Buffett went to Fort Worth, met John Roach and John Justin — and bought the company for $570M cash.

John Justin had been forced to retire due to severe health problems (died February 2001); he had groomed two outstanding managers who run their divisions autonomously.

## Buffett's "Clicks and Bricks" Joke

> "I can't resist pointing out that Berkshire — whose top management has long been mired in the 19th century — is now one of the very few authentic 'clicks-and-bricks' businesses around. We went into 2000 with GEICO doing significant business on the Internet, and then we added Acme. You can bet this move by Berkshire is making them sweat in Silicon Valley." — 2000 Letter
