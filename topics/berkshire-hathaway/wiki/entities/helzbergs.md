## Overview

A chain of 134 mall-based diamond jewelry stores acquired by Berkshire in 1995 via stock exchange. The deal originated from a chance sidewalk encounter — Barnett Helzberg Jr. approached Buffett at 58th Street and Fifth Avenue in New York City in May 1994.

## Key Facts

- **Acquired:** 1995 via Berkshire stock exchange
- **Founder:** Barnett Helzberg Jr. (third generation; grandfather founded 1915)
- **Operations:** 134 stores, $282M annual sales
- **CEO:** Jeff Comment (non-family professional manager)
- **Deal origin:** Helzberg approached Buffett on a Manhattan sidewalk after recognizing him; the stock-for-stock exchange avoided taxable gains for the seller

## Significance

**[1995 Letter]** Helzberg's illustrates the "have-to-be-smart-every-day" retail challenge — but with excellent management (Jeff Comment) and a focused niche (diamonds in malls). The sidewalk-encounter origin story demonstrates how Berkshire's reputation generates unsolicited deal flow from motivated sellers who want cultural preservation.

Buffett classifies retailing as an "express ticket to bankruptcy" when management falters — making the quality of operators like Jeff Comment the decisive investment criterion rather than the industry structure.
