## Overview

Star Furniture is a Houston-area home furnishings retailer acquired by Berkshire Hathaway on July 1, 1997. The business had been in the Wolff family since 1924. At acquisition it operated 12 stores across the Houston metropolitan area.

## Key Facts

- **Acquired:** July 1, 1997
- **Founder/CEO:** Melvyn Wolff (owner and operator), with sister Shirley Toomin as partner
- **Location:** Houston metropolitan area; 12 stores at acquisition
- **Family history:** In the Wolff family since 1924
- **Referral source:** Irv Blumkin (Nebraska Furniture Mart) recommended the business to Buffett — a peer referral from within the Berkshire family of furniture retailers
- **Seller conduct:** At closing, the sellers made large special payments to all employees from their own personal pockets — a detail Buffett highlights as emblematic of the kind of owner Berkshire seeks to acquire from

## Investment Thesis

Star Furniture exemplifies Berkshire's acquisition archetype: a dominant regional retailer with a long owner-operator history, simple economics, and management that stayed post-acquisition. The Irv Blumkin referral echoes the Nebraska Furniture Mart model — Buffett explicitly relies on existing owners to identify comparable businesses in other markets.
