## Overview

Precision Castparts Corp. is the world's premier supplier of aerospace components, acquired by Berkshire for more than $32B in cash in January 2016. Led by CEO Mark Donegan, PCC operates 162 plants in 13 countries with 30,466 employees. Most products are original equipment destined for large aircraft, delivered under multi-year contracts.

## Key Facts

- **Acquired:** January 2016 (contracted 2015)
- **Price:** $32B+ (all cash) — Berkshire's largest-ever all-cash acquisition
- **CEO:** Mark Donegan — "the da Vinci of his craft" alongside Jacob Harpaz (IMC/ISCAR)
- **Employees:** 30,466
- **Operations:** 162 plants, 13 countries
- **Primary market:** Aerospace components (original equipment and spares)
- **Identified by:** Todd Combs brought PCC to Buffett's attention; "one of my best moves"

## Significance

PCC elevates the **Powerhouse Five** to the **Powerhouse Six** beginning in 2016. Buffett describes PCC as fitting "perfectly into the Berkshire model" and notes it will "substantially increase our normalized per-share earning power." The acquisition demonstrates several [capital allocation](../principles/capital-allocation.md) principles: (1) all-cash purchase avoids shareholder dilution, (2) existing management retained, (3) Berkshire provides unlimited capital for bolt-on acquisitions.

PCC also validates the role of Todd Combs and Ted Weschler beyond investment management: Combs identified and educated Buffett on the business, exemplifying the teaching model where investment and operating experience reinforce each other[^1].

With PCC, Berkshire owns **10¼ companies** that would individually populate the Fortune 500 as standalone businesses.

### 2020: $11B Writedown — "I Paid Too Much"

The 2020 letter contains Buffett's most candid acquisition-error confession: **$11B writedown** on PCC (and other subsidiaries, "almost entirely PCC"). The error: overpaying for normalized profit potential. \"No one misled me in any way — I was simply too optimistic about PCC's normalized profit potential.\" Aerospace downturn accelerated the recognition, but the root cause was valuation misjudgment at purchase.

Key distinctions: (1) PCC itself remains excellent — \"the best in its business,\" (2) Donegan \"pours the same energy into the business\" as pre-acquisition, (3) the error was in [four-filters](../principles/four-filters.md) filter (4) — price — not in filters (1)-(3). \"PCC is far from my first error of that sort. But it's a big one\"[^2].

### 2025: Aerospace Recovery, $2.4B Operating Cash, and Jenkintown Fire

**[2025 Letter]** PCC has "worked through the most challenging part of that period" — aerospace recovery driving results. Net operating cash flows: **$2.4B** (vs. $0.9B avg in 2021-22 and $1.7B in 2015, last pre-acquisition year).

The **Jenkintown, Pennsylvania fire** (February 2025) destroyed a facility producing **700+ sole-sourced parts** critical to major aerospace customers. Mark Donegan's team responded by redistributing production across US and international sites "without compromising safety, quality, or delivery standards. No customer experienced a production line stoppage."

Abel presents PCC's fire response as the exemplar of operational excellence: "decentralized leadership, clear accountability, and exceptional execution under pressure." The episode also demonstrated PCC's competitive moat — a facility producing 700+ sole-sourced parts is irreplaceable, and the ability to redistribute across a global network is the competitive advantage[^3].
