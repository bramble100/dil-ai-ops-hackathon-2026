## Overview

The nation's leading producer of commercial and industrial insulation, acquired by Berkshire for approximately $1.8B in December 2000 (agreement) / February 27, 2001 (close). JM emerged from an asbestos-related bankruptcy in the 1980s; the trust that controlled the company sold it to Berkshire after an LBO fell through at the last minute.

## Key Facts

- **Acquired:** February 27, 2001 (agreed December 2000)
- **Price:** ~$1.8B cash (no financing contingencies)
- **CEO:** Jerry Henry (had announced retirement; Buffett convinced him to stay)
- **Sales:** >$2B annually
- **Positions:** Leading commercial/industrial insulation; major roofing systems; engineered products
- **History:** Long asbestos manufacturing history → bankruptcy 1982 → trust became controlling shareholder → LBO fell through December 8, 2000 → Berkshire called Monday December 11 → tentative board approval December 12

## Acquisition Story

On Friday, December 8, the LBO deal collapsed (financing could not be secured). The following Monday, Buffett and Munger called Bob Felise, chairman of the trust, and made an all-cash offer with no financing contingencies. Tuesday: trustees tentatively accepted. One week later: contract signed. The ability to move decisively without financing contingencies — a defining Berkshire acquisition advantage — secured this deal.

## First Year Results (2001)

Contributed to the ~$659M pre-tax combined result from the three large 2000-cohort acquisitions (Shaw + Johns Manville + Benjamin Moore). Jerry Henry remained as CEO despite prior retirement plans.
