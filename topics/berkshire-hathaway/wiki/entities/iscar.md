## Overview

ISCAR is an Israeli cutting-tool manufacturer and Berkshire's first international acquisition. Eitan Wertheimer's 1¼-page letter to Buffett initiated the deal, which closed July 5, 2006 for $4B (80% stake). The Wertheimer family retained 20%.

## Key Facts

- **Acquired:** July 5, 2006
- **Consideration:** $4B cash for 80%
- **Remaining 20%:** Wertheimer family (valued partner)
- **Chairman:** Eitan Wertheimer
- **CEO:** Jacob Harpaz
- **CFO:** Danny Goldman
- **Business:** Small, consumable cutting tools used with large machine tools; operations in 61 countries
- **Value proposition:** "Makes money because it enables its customers to make more money"
- **Origin:** Eitan's October 25, 2005 letter; November 2005 Omaha visit; deal after Buffett educated himself on international tax/jurisdictional matters

## Significance

ISCAR marks three firsts for Berkshire:

1. **First international acquisition** — Buffett had bought foreign stocks but never a business based outside the US
2. **"Buyer of choice" goes global** — Eitan's letter explicitly identified Berkshire as "the ideal home for ISCAR," extending Berkshire's reputation beyond US borders
3. **Consumable product model** — cutting tools are small, consumable, and generate recurring revenue when they make customers' machines more productive; "a business without magic except for that imparted by the people who run it"

Buffett and five Berkshire associates visited ISCAR in Israel in September 2006 and were "never more impressed with any operation." The visit confirmed that "brains and energy are ubiquitous" at ISCAR[^1].

## 2007: Global Expansion — Dalian and Korea

**[2007 Letter]** ISCAR continues rapid international growth. A new plant opens in **Dalian, China** — extending the manufacturing footprint into Asia. The company visits **Korea** to explore further expansion. Under Eitan Wertheimer and Jacob Harpaz, the operating model of consumable cutting tools proves highly adaptable across geographies. Buffett continues to marvel at the management quality: "meeting the Wertheimer family and learning about ISCAR" was a 2006 highlight that keeps paying dividends[^2].

## 2008: Tungaloy Acquisition

**[2008 Letter]** ISCAR acquires **Tungaloy**, a Japanese cutting-tool manufacturer, extending its Asian presence significantly. The acquisition fits the ISCAR model: small consumable tools, global distribution, recurring demand. With operations now spanning Israel, China, Japan, Korea, and dozens of other countries, ISCAR's global platform is among the most diversified in Berkshire's portfolio[^3].
