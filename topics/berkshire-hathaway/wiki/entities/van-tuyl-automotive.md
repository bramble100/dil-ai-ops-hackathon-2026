## Overview

Van Tuyl Automotive Group, the fifth-largest US auto dealership group, was acquired by Berkshire Hathaway in 2014. The company operates 78 dealerships with approximately $9B in annual sales. Larry Van Tuyl built the business over 62 years, and upon deciding on a permanent home, chose Berkshire. The deal required approval from each auto manufacturer whose franchises were included — a unique regulatory hurdle.

## Key Facts

- **Acquired:** 2014 (contracted; closed early 2015)
- **Dealerships:** 78
- **Annual sales:** ~$9B
- **Ranking:** 5th-largest US auto dealership group
- **Founder:** Larry Van Tuyl (62 years building the business)
- **CEO:** Jeff Rachor
- **Structure:** Owner-partner model — local managers run dealerships with significant autonomy

## Significance

Van Tuyl exemplifies the "permanent home" acquisition thesis that Buffett articulated in the 2014 Golden Anniversary letter. Larry Van Tuyl had three choices: sell to a competitor (who would fire his people), sell to private equity (who would pile on debt and resell), or sell to Berkshire for permanent ownership. He chose Berkshire.

The acquisition also demonstrates Berkshire's increasing scale: with Van Tuyl, Berkshire now owns **9½ companies** that would be in the Fortune 500 if independent. "That leaves 490½ fish in the sea. Our lines are out."

The owner-partner management model — where local dealership managers operate with entrepreneurial autonomy within the Berkshire framework — mirrors the extreme subsidiary CEO autonomy that Munger identified as element #2 of the Berkshire system in his 2014 independent letter.
