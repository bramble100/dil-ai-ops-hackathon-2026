## Overview

International Dairy Queen (IDQ) is a global fast-food franchise operator acquired by Berkshire Hathaway. The deal was agreed in late 1997 and closed in early 1998. IDQ operates the Dairy Queen, Orange Julius, and Karmelkorn brands across 23 countries.

## Key Facts

- **Agreed:** Late 1997; **Closed:** Early 1998
- **Scale:** 5,792 DQ stores in 23 countries; 409 Orange Julius locations; 43 Karmelkorn locations
- **Management:** John Mooty and Mike Sullivan as co-CEOs at acquisition
- **Deal structure:** Buffett tilted consideration toward cash (preferred cash payment). Notably, only 45% of shareholders elected the cash option — suggesting most preferred Berkshire stock, which itself is a data point on Berkshire's perceived quality
- **Buffett/Munger edge:** Both are longtime DQ customers and product enthusiasts — Buffett explicitly cites his personal consumption of Dilly Bars as evidence of his understanding of the franchise's customer loyalty

## Investment Thesis

DQ is a consumer franchise with decades of brand recognition and repeat-purchase economics. It fits Berkshire's "The Inevitables" framework — a brand so embedded in consumer habits that it persists across economic cycles. The owner-operator franchise model generates capital-light recurring revenues. Buffett's willingness to structure consideration toward cash (rather than issuing stock) reflects his ongoing discipline around equity issuance.
