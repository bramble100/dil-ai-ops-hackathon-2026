# Scott & Fetzer

## Overview

A diversified manufacturer acquired by Berkshire in 1986 (announced late 1985) for approximately $320M (plus assumption of ~$90M in existing debt). Revenues ~$700M across 17 businesses. Ralph Schey had served as CEO for nine years at the time of acquisition.

## Key Facts

- **Purchase price:** ~$320M equity + ~$90M debt assumed
- **Revenue:** ~$700M annually
- **Business units:** 17, including World Book Inc., Kirby (vacuum cleaners), Campbell Hausfeld, Wayne (water systems)
- **Flagship:** World Book Inc. (~40% of sales) — #1 encyclopedia by a factor of 2x over nearest competitor
- **CEO:** Ralph Schey (9 years at acquisition)
- **Acquisition timeline:** Announced as a candidate since early 1984; Ivan Boesky made a 1985 offer; an employee stock ownership plan (ESOP) was proposed and failed in court; Buffett wrote a letter to Schey on Oct 10, 1985; dinner Oct 22; contract signed the following week

## Significance

Scott & Fetzer illustrates several key [principles](../principles/four-filters.md): (1) business with strong economics (World Book's dominant market position), (2) able management (Schey's long tenure), (3) available at a fair price after a failed ESOP attempt scared away other bidders. The speed of the transaction — letter to contract in under three weeks — reflects Berkshire's advantage as a buyer who does not require extensive due diligence, multiple committee approvals, or investment banker intermediation.

Buffett notes: "We made no study of the industry or of its competitors, nor did we have discussion with investment bankers or anyone knowledgeable about the company."
