## Overview

Gregory E. Abel is the CEO of Berkshire Hathaway, succeeding Warren Buffett. He wrote his first annual shareholder letter in February 2026 covering the 2025 fiscal year — the first letter not authored by Buffett in 60 years.

## Key Facts

- **Current role:** CEO of Berkshire Hathaway (succeeded Buffett)
- **Previous role:** Vice Chairman — Non-Insurance Operations (from 2018); CEO of Berkshire Hathaway Energy (joined as CalEnergy, pre-Berkshire)
- **Background:** Moved to Omaha in 1992 to join CalEnergy (then unaffiliated with Berkshire), partly owned by Peter Kiewit Sons' and chaired by Walter Scott, Jr.
- **Named successor:** Formally identified alongside Ajit Jain as "world-leading" in the 2014 Golden Anniversary letter; appointed Vice Chairman in 2017
- **First letter:** February 28, 2026, covering fiscal year 2025
- **Key achievement:** Codified Berkshire's culture and six foundational values for the first time in writing

## Significance

Abel represents the most consequential leadership transition in Berkshire's history. His 2025 letter establishes the post-Buffett operating framework through six codified foundational values:

1. **Decentralized Model** — autonomy grounded in deserved trust; no bureaucracy; accountability
2. **Integrity** — "earned, re-earned, and maintained daily"; Salomon testimony still resonant
3. **Financial Strength** — fortress balance sheet as strategic asset; $370B+ cash/Treasuries
4. **Capital Discipline** — five investment principles; OxyChem and Bell Labs as first acquisitions under Abel leadership
5. **Risk Management** — CEO as Chief Risk Officer; Ajit Jain "wrote the playbook"
6. **Operational Excellence** — PCC Jenkintown fire as proof; safety first; customer focus

Abel's letter is notably more structured and corporate in tone than Buffett's folksy style, with explicit business-segment reporting and performance metrics. Key signals:

- **Candor maintained:** Shaw's "self-inflicted" problems called out; BNSF's margin gap acknowledged; Kraft Heinz called "disappointing"
- **New leadership additions:** Adam Johnson (president, consumer/service/retailing — 32 companies); Rebecca Liebert (Lubrizol + OxyChem); Marc Hamburg retiring (Chuck Chang successor); Mike O'Sullivan (first General Counsel)
- **Warren's continuing role:** Chairman, "in the office five days a week," available for insurance, operations, and capital allocation; his shares going entirely to philanthropy

> "Charlie's comment on May 1, 2021, that 'Greg will keep the culture' will forever resonate with me." — 2025 Letter
