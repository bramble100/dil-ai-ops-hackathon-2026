## Overview

A 117-year-old paint manufacturer with thousands of independent dealers, acquired by Berkshire for $1B cash in December 2000. The acquisition originated through Bob Mundheim (a director) who called Buffett to ask if Berkshire might be interested — the type of "cold-call" acquisition that increasingly flows to Berkshire by reputation.

## Key Facts

- **Acquired:** December 2000
- **Price:** $1B cash
- **History:** 117 years of paint manufacturing
- **CEOs:** Richard Roob (past) and Yvan Dupuy (present) met with Buffett and Munger in late August; Berkshire made a $1B cash offer on the spot
- **Distribution:** Thousands of independent dealers — a vital distribution asset

## Acquisition Story

Bob Mundheim, a director of Benjamin Moore who had been general counsel at Salomon during difficult times, called Buffett to ask about Berkshire's interest. Buffett: "My answer was 'Tell me more.'" In late August, Buffett and Munger met with the CEOs, liked the business, liked the management, and made a $1B cash offer on the spot. Board approved in October; deal closed in December.

## First Year Results (2001)

Contributed to the ~$659M pre-tax combined result from the three large 2000-cohort acquisitions. Yvan Dupuy remained as CEO — his first full year results praised.
