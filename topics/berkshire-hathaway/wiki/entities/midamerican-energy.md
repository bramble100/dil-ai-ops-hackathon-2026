## Overview

MidAmerican Energy Holdings Company is a regulated electric utility acquired by Berkshire Hathaway in 1999 for approximately $2B. It was Berkshire's first major utility investment and marked the beginning of a significant capital allocation category. Due to the Public Utility Holding Company Act of 1935 (PUHCA), Berkshire's voting power is constrained to less than 10% of the utility's shares even though its economic interest is approximately 76%.

## Key Facts

- **Acquired:** 1999
- **Consideration:** ~$2B (mix of 11% fixed income securities and equity)
- **Economic interest:** ~76% (majority economic ownership)
- **Voting interest:** <10% (constrained by PUHCA 1935 which limits utility holding company voting concentration)
- **CEO:** David Sokol (named CEO at acquisition; later became a leading candidate to succeed Buffett before his departure from Berkshire in 2011)
- **Facilitator:** Walter Scott Jr. (longtime Buffett friend and board member; introduced the opportunity)
- **Business:** Regulated electric and gas utility serving Iowa and surrounding states; predictable cash flows from rate-regulated operations

## Investment Rationale

MidAmerican represented a large-capital deployment opportunity in a business with predictable, regulated economics. Buffett was attracted to utilities because:

1. Regulated returns are predictable even if not spectacular
2. The capital base is enormous — utilities can absorb billions of Berkshire's retained earnings
3. The regulatory framework creates an effective moat: competitors cannot enter without regulatory approval

The PUHCA voting constraint was not a deterrent — Berkshire's economic ownership is what matters, and the regulatory structure governing utilities provides governance protections that substitute for voting control.

## 2000-2001 Operations

**[2000 Letter]** MidAmerican closes (acquisition was agreed 1999, completed 2000). Due to PUHCA regulatory constraints, Berkshire performs only a "one-line" consolidation — if fully consolidated, Berkshire's 2000 revenues would have been $5B greater (though net income unchanged). Pre-tax earnings: $197M (2000).

**[2001 Letter]** Strong year: pre-tax earnings $600M (vs. $197M in 2000, which was a partial year). MidAmerican swapped some English properties for Yorkshire Electric (2.1 million customers). Now serving **3.6 million customers in the UK** — the 2nd largest electric utility in the UK. Also has significant operations in Iowa and major generating facilities in California and the Philippines.

Hidden asset: MidAmerican also owns the **2nd-largest residential real estate brokerage in the US** (built by Ron Peltier). Market-share leaders in multiple large Midwest cities; recently acquired firms in Atlanta and Southern California. Handled ~106,000 transactions worth ~$20B in properties annually in 2001.

Goodwill amortization charge will disappear under SFAS 142 (new GAAP rule eliminating mandatory goodwill amortization), which will significantly increase MidAmerican's reported earnings.
