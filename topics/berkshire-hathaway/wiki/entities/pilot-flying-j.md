## Overview

Pilot Flying J is North America's largest operator of travel centers, with annual revenue exceeding $20B. Berkshire acquired a **38.6% partnership interest** in 2017, with an agreement to increase ownership to approximately **80% in 2023**. The Haslam family retains operational control. The acquisition exemplifies Berkshire's preferred deal structure: partnership with proven owner-operators rather than auction-driven transactions.

## Key Facts

- **Acquired:** 2017 (38.6% partnership; ~80% by 2023)
- **Business:** Truck stops and travel centers across North America
- **Revenue:** $20B+ annually
- **Founder/operator:** Haslam family (retains minority stake and management)
- **Structure:** Partnership — not a hostile bid, not an auction

## Significance

The Pilot Flying J deal illustrates several Berkshire principles:

- **[Four Filters](../principles/four-filters.md) application:** Simple, understandable business (travel centers at prime highway locations); durable competitive advantage (network effects of locations + scale); proven management (Haslam family built it from scratch); reasonable price through partnership rather than competitive bidding.
- **[Capital Allocation](../principles/capital-allocation.md):** Patient, staged acquisition — starting with minority stake, scaling to control. This avoids the "acquisition-mania" Buffett critiques in the same letter.
- **Anti-auction model:** Buffett's preferred approach — seller chooses Berkshire as a permanent home for the business, rather than selling to the highest bidder.
