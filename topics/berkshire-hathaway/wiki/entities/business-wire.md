## Overview

Business Wire is a leading press release distribution service, agreed to be acquired by Berkshire in 2005 (closed early 2006). Founded in 1961 by Lorry Lokey, the company serves 25,000 clients in 150 countries. The deal originated when Lokey (age ~78) wrote to Buffett after reading a Wall Street Journal article about Berkshire's acquisition criteria.

## Key Facts

- **Founded:** 1961 by Lorry Lokey
- **CEO:** Cathy Baron Tamraz
- **Clients:** 25,000 in 150 countries
- **Industry position:** "Gold standard for business wire services"
- **Deal origin:** Lokey wrote Buffett after a WSJ article about acquisition criteria — another example of Berkshire's annual reports and media presence functioning as permanent deal-sourcing advertisements
- **Motivation:** Lokey, at age 78, sought a permanent home for the business he built over 44 years

## Significance

Business Wire demonstrates the recurring pattern of founder-operators seeking Berkshire as a permanent home for their life's work. Like Pete Liegl (Forest River), Lokey could have sold to private equity or strategic acquirers but chose Berkshire for the promise that the business would not be dismembered, its culture would be preserved, and its CEO (Tamraz) would continue running it independently.

The acquisition also illustrates Berkshire's passive deal-sourcing machine: the combination of Buffett's annual letters, media coverage, and decades of reputation attracts sellers who value permanence over price maximization[^1].
