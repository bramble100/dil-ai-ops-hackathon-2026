## Overview

A major investment bank. Berkshire's largest single investment in 1987 — a $700M convertible preferred stock position.

## Key Facts

- **Investment:** $700M in 9% convertible preferred stock (1987)
- **Conversion terms:** Convertible into common at $38/share after three years; if not converted, redeemed ratably over five years beginning October 31, 1995
- **CEO:** John Gutfreund — Buffett and Munger "like, admire and trust John"
- **Prior relationship:** Gutfreund played a key role in GEICO's escape from near-bankruptcy in 1976; repeatedly steered clients away from unwise transactions even when it cost Salomon fees
- **Yearend 1987 valuation:** Carried at 98% of par ($14M below cost) per Charlie Munger's reasoning
- **Investment banking:** Salomon served as Berkshire's banker for the $250M debenture issuance

## Significance

The Salomon preferred represents Buffett's willingness to use convertible preferred structures for industries where he has "no special insights regarding direction or future profitability." The unpredictability of investment banking economics made a common stock position inappropriate — the preferred provides downside protection via the coupon and redemption schedule, with upside participation via conversion.

**[1987 Letter]** "Such service-above-self behavior is far from automatic in Wall Street" — the investment was driven primarily by trust in Gutfreund's character, not by analysis of investment banking economics.

### 1988-1989 Updates

**[1988 Letter]** The $700M preferred position continues. Admiration for Gutfreund reinforced. Salomon participated in the RJR Nabisco leveraged buyout contest — a transaction Buffett observed firsthand through his Salomon board seat, informing his arbitrage analysis[^2].

**[1989 Letter]** Salomon is the original template for the convertible preferred strategy now extended to Gillette, USAir, and Champion. Buffett reaffirms that the preferred structure suits industries where he lacks "special insights regarding direction or future profitability" — the preferred coupon provides an acceptable floor while conversion offers upside participation. Gutfreund's integrity continues to be the thesis[^3].
