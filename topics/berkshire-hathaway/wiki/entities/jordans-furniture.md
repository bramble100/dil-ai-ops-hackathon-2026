## Overview

Jordan's Furniture is a New England home furnishings retailer acquired by Berkshire Hathaway on November 13, 1999. A family business since 1927, Jordan's was run by brothers Barry and Eliot Tatelman. Its defining characteristic was industry-leading sales per square foot achieved through the "shoppertainment" model — integrating entertainment attractions into its showroom floors.

## Key Facts

- **Acquired:** November 13, 1999
- **Owners:** Barry and Eliot Tatelman (brothers; third-generation family business since 1927)
- **Location:** New England (Massachusetts-based flagship operations)
- **Performance:** Highest sales per square foot of any major US furniture retailer
- **Shoppertainment:** Jordan's embedded entertainment experiences (IMAX theaters, motion simulators, themed environments) into its store floors, driving foot traffic and dwell time that translated into exceptional sales density
- **Seller conduct at closing:** The Tatelman brothers paid approximately $9M in special bonuses to all employees out of their own personal pockets — a gesture Buffett highlights as characteristic of the owner-operators Berkshire seeks

## Investment Thesis

Jordan's illustrates the Berkshire furniture-retailer collection thesis: dominant regional operators with unusual owner talent and deep customer loyalty that national chains cannot replicate. The shoppertainment model created a differentiated experience moat. The Tatelman brothers' $9M employee bonus echoes the Star Furniture and Nebraska Furniture Mart acquisition patterns — owners who genuinely care about their employees make for better long-term business partners.
