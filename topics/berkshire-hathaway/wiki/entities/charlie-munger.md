## Overview

Vice Chairman of Berkshire Hathaway and Chairman of Blue Chip Stamps. Buffett's intellectual partner — "Irrespective of titles, Charlie and I work as partners in managing all controlled companies."

## Key Facts

- **Role:** Vice Chairman of Berkshire Hathaway; Chairman of Wesco Financial (replaced Louie Vincenti late 1983); formerly Chairman of Blue Chip Stamps
- **Partnership:** "To almost a sinful degree, we enjoy our work as managing partners" [1981 Letter]
- **[1982 Letter]** "Charlie and I are interchangeable in business decisions. Distance impedes us not at all: we've always found a telephone call to be more productive than a half-day committee meeting."
- **[1983 Letter]** Co-author of the 13 Owner's Manual principles: "Charlie Munger and I think of our shareholders as owner-partners, and of ourselves as managing partners."
- **Notable contributions (1977-1983):**
  - Conceived the shareholder-designated charitable contributions program (1981)
  - Co-architect of the partnership philosophy codified in the 1983 Owner's Manual
  - Replaced Louie Vincenti as Wesco Financial Chairman (late 1983)

## Significance

In these early letters, Munger appears primarily as a named partner rather than a quoted thinker. His intellectual contributions — mental models, multidisciplinary thinking, the "wonderful business at a fair price" formulation — will emerge more explicitly in later letters. But his fingerprints are already on key decisions: the shareholder-designated contributions innovation and the strategic management of Blue Chip/Wesco operations.
