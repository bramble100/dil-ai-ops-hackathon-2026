## Overview

Lubrizol Corporation is a specialty chemical company acquired by Berkshire in September 2011 for approximately $4.5B. Under CEO James Hambrick (since 2004), Lubrizol grew pre-tax profits from $147M to $1,085M — a 7x increase that attracted Berkshire's attention. Lubrizol produces specialty additives for lubricants, fuels, and other industrial applications.

## Key Facts

- **Acquired:** September 16, 2011
- **Purchase price:** ~$4.5B (all cash)
- **CEO:** James Hambrick (since 2004)
- **Pre-tax profits at acquisition:** $1,085M
- **Products:** Specialty chemical additives for lubricants, fuels, industrial applications
- **Bolt-on acquisitions:** Three agreed immediately after close, totaling $493M

## Significance

Lubrizol joins BNSF, Iscar, Marmon, and MidAmerican as one of the **"Fabulous Five"** (later **"Powerhouse Five"**) — the group earning >$9B pre-tax in 2011 and $10.1B in 2012. Only MidAmerican was owned seven years before the 2011 letter; the rapid assembly of five $1B+ earners through acquisition demonstrates Berkshire's capital deployment capabilities at scale.

James Hambrick's track record (7x profit growth) exemplifies the Berkshire acquisition model: find a business with proven management, buy it at a sensible price, and let the managers continue running it autonomously[^1][^2].

### 2025: OxyChem Responsibility

**[2025 Letter]** Rebecca Liebert, Lubrizol's CEO, assumes additional responsibility for **OxyChem** (acquired by Berkshire from Occidental Petroleum). This consolidation of chemical operations under one leader signals Lubrizol's importance as Berkshire's chemical platform. Liebert's expanded role demonstrates the trust placed in her management capabilities[^3].
