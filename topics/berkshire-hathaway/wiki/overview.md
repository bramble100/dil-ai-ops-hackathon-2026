# Buffett's Letters — Investor Education Wiki

> "It's far better to buy a wonderful company at a fair price than a fair company at a wonderful price." — 1989 Letter

This wiki distills **49 years of Warren Buffett's annual shareholder letters** (1977-2025) into an interconnected knowledge base. The goal is not corporate history — it's **investor education**. Every page answers a variation of: _What would Buffett think about this situation, and why?_

## What Changed and What Didn't

Across 49 years of letters, some things evolved and some remained constant:

**Constants (1977-2025):**

- Four selection filters (understandable, durable, honest, cheap)
- Concentration over diversification
- Management integrity as non-negotiable
- Long-term holding; "favorite holding period is forever"
- Candid admission of mistakes
- Hostility to leverage and financial engineering

**Evolutions:**

- From cigar butts to wonderful companies (1970s → 1980s)
- From pure investing to operating conglomerate (1990s → 2000s)
- From book value to operating earnings as the yardstick (2018)
- From domestic to international (ISCAR 2006, Japanese companies 2020s)
- From "no tech" to Apple as largest holding (2016-2018)
- From Buffett to Abel (2025)
