# Buffett's Greatest Quotes

Memorable passages organized by theme. Each quote includes year, context, and link to the relevant principle page.

---

## Business Quality & Moats

> "One of the lessons your management has learned — and, unfortunately, sometimes re-learned — is the importance of being in businesses where tailwinds prevail rather than headwinds." — 1977 Letter
>
> Context: Contrasting textile struggles with insurance success. → [Business Quality](principles/business-quality.md)

> "The textile industry illustrates in textbook style how producers of relatively undifferentiated goods in capital intensive businesses must earn inadequate returns except under conditions of tight supply or real shortage." — 1978 Letter
>
> Context: Explaining why competitive improvements in textiles are futile. → [Textile Operations](case-studies/textile-operations.md)

> "Turnarounds seldom turn, and the same energies and talent are much better employed in a good business purchased at a fair price than in a poor business purchased at a bargain price." — 1979 Letter
>
> Context: Reflecting on the Waumbec Mills acquisition mistake. → [Business Quality](principles/business-quality.md)

> "When a management with a reputation for brilliance tackles a business with a reputation for poor fundamental economics, it is the reputation of the business that remains intact." — 1980 Letter
>
> Context: Generalizing from turnaround failures. → [Business Quality](principles/business-quality.md)

## Valuation & Investing

> "We select our marketable equity securities in much the same way we would evaluate a business for acquisition in its entirety." — 1977 Letter
>
> Context: Introducing the four stock selection criteria. → [Four Filters](principles/four-filters.md)

> "We paid less than 100 cents on the dollar for the best company in the business, when far more than 100 cents on the dollar is being paid for mediocre companies in corporate transactions." — 1978 Letter
>
> Context: On buying SAFECO stock below book value. → [Capital Allocation](principles/capital-allocation.md)

## Capital Allocation & Acquisitions

> "Investors can always buy toads at the going price for toads. If investors instead bankroll princesses who wish to pay double for the right to kiss the toad, those kisses had better pack some real dynamite. We've observed many kisses but very few miracles." — 1981 Letter
>
> Context: On overpaying in acquisitions. → [Capital Allocation](principles/capital-allocation.md)

> "We would rather buy 10% of Wonderful Business T at X per share than 100% of T at 2X per share." — 1981 Letter
>
> Context: Preferring minority ownership at fair prices over control at high prices. → [Capital Allocation](principles/capital-allocation.md)

> "If a fine business is selling in the market place for far less than intrinsic value, what more certain or more profitable utilization of capital can there be than significant enlargement of the interests of all owners at that bargain price?" — 1980 Letter
>
> Context: On share buybacks. → [Capital Allocation](principles/capital-allocation.md)

## Owner Earnings & Accounting

> "If a tree grows in a forest partially owned by us, but we don't record the growth in our financial statements, we still own part of the tree." — 1980 Letter
>
> Context: On unreported retained earnings of investees. → [Owner Earnings](principles/owner-earnings.md)

> "'Earnings per share' will rise constantly on a dormant savings account or on a U.S. Savings Bond bearing a fixed rate of return simply because 'earnings' are continuously plowed back and added to the capital base. Thus, even a 'stopped clock' can look like a growth stock if the dividend payout ratio is low." — 1979 Letter
>
> Context: On why EPS growth is a misleading metric. → [Return on Equity](principles/return-on-equity.md)

## Inflation

> "Inflation acts as a gigantic corporate tapeworm. That tapeworm preemptively consumes its requisite daily diet of investment dollars regardless of the health of the host organism." — 1981 Letter
>
> Context: On inflation's destruction of capital-intensive business returns. → [Inflation and Investing](principles/inflation-and-investing.md)

> "You may feel richer, but you won't eat richer." — 1980 Letter
>
> Context: The hamburger test for real investment returns. → [Inflation and Investing](principles/inflation-and-investing.md)

> "Like virginity, a stable price level seems capable of maintenance, but not of restoration." — 1981 Letter
>
> Context: On the intractability of inflation. → [Inflation and Investing](principles/inflation-and-investing.md)

## Management & Character

> "From the time we first met, Gene shot straight 100% of the time — the only behavior pattern he had within him." — 1980 Letter
>
> Context: Tribute to Gene Abegg, Illinois National Bank. → Integrity in management

> "It is a real pleasure to work with managers who enjoy coming to work each morning and, once there, instinctively and unerringly think like owners." — 1978 Letter
>
> Context: On Berkshire's elderly but outstanding managers. → Management quality

## Humor & Self-Deprecation

> "We neglected the Noah principle: predicting rain doesn't count, building arks does." — 1981 Letter
>
> Context: Admitting that understanding economics intellectually didn't translate to timely action.

> "Several minor adjustments to that opinion — now aggregating approximately 180 degrees — have since been required." — 1981 Letter
>
> Context: On his wrong prediction about the aluminum business.
