## Corporate Structure

Rheinmetall AG is the **management holding company** (Managementholding) of the Rheinmetall Group. It sets long-term strategic direction and corporate policy, and provides centralized services including:
- Finance and treasury
- HR
- Legal, tax, and compliance
- Internal audit
- Corporate sustainability
- Communications and investor relations

[Source: AR 2025, p.22]

## Reporting Segments (2025)

Rheinmetall's external reporting structure comprises three defense segments plus one discontinued segment:

### Continuing Operations (Defense)
1. **Vehicle Systems Europe** - armored vehicles and logistics vehicles for European customers
2. **Vehicle Systems International** - same portfolio for non-European markets (Australia, Americas, etc.)
3. **Weapon and Ammunition** - weapons, ammunition, air defense, force protection
4. **Electronic Solutions** - sensors, C4ISR, cyber defense, simulation, training

Note: Vehicle Systems is split into Europe and International on the external reporting level but managed as one division. [Source: AR 2025, p.5]

### Discontinued Operations
- **Power Systems** - civilian automotive/industrial components (classified IFRS 5, 17 Dec 2025)

See [Automotive to Defense Pivot](../concepts/automotive-to-defense-pivot.md) for the history.

## Global Footprint

As of 2022, Rheinmetall operated across 138 countries with 132 locations globally. Key geographies:

- **Germany (HQ):** Dusseldorf, Unterluess, Kassel, Kiel, Bremen, Oberndorf, and many more
- **Americas:** USA (Auburn Hills MI, Sterling Heights MI, Marinette WI), Canada (Ottawa), Brazil, Mexico
- **Europe:** UK, Netherlands, Italy, Switzerland, Norway, Romania, Lithuania, Hungary, Austria
- **Asia-Pacific:** Australia (Brisbane MilVehCoE), South Korea, Japan, South Africa

[Source: AR 2022, pp.3-4]
