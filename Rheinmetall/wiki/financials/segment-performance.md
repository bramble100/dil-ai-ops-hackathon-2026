# Segment Performance

## Current Structure (2025)

Since December 2025, Rheinmetall operates as a pure defense company with three reporting segments (plus discontinued civilian Power Systems). Each segment has a distinct financial profile.

### Vehicle Systems (2024-2025)

| Metric | 2025 | 2024 | Change |
|--------|------|------|--------|
| Revenue (MioEUR) | 4,992 | 3,790 | +31.7% |
| Operating Result (MioEUR) | 583 | 425 | +37.2% |
| Operating Margin (%) | 11.7 | 11.2 | +0.5pp |
| Backlog - Rheinmetall (MioEUR) | 24,391 | 21,881 | +11.5% |
| Order Intake (MioEUR) | 8,608 | 7,107 | +21.1% |
| Investment (MioEUR) | 199 | 141 | +41.1% |
| Free Cash Flow (MioEUR) | 426 | 275 | +54.9% |
| Employees (FTE) | 9,363 | 8,301 | +12.8% |

[Source: Extraction notes, AR 2025]

**Profile:** The largest segment by revenue. Produces armored vehicles (Lynx IFV, Boxer CRV, Leopard 2 components), logistics trucks, and turret systems. Revenue driven in 2025 by increased deliveries of Wechselladersysteme (swap-body trucks) and ramp-up of tactical vehicle programs (Boxer MIV, Schwerer Waffentrager). Margins of ~11-12% are moderate for defense, reflecting the capital-intensive nature of vehicle manufacturing and long program lifecycles.

### Weapon and Ammunition (2024-2025)

| Metric | 2025 | 2024 | Change |
|--------|------|------|--------|
| Revenue (MioEUR) | 3,532 | 2,783 | +26.9% |
| Operating Result (MioEUR) | 1,037 | 790 | +31.3% |
| Operating Margin (%) | **29.3** | **28.4** | +0.9pp |
| Backlog - Rheinmetall (MioEUR) | 24,018 | 20,529 | +17.0% |
| Order Intake (MioEUR) | 6,736 | 6,237 | +8.0% |
| Investment (MioEUR) | **402** | 304 | +32.2% |
| Free Cash Flow (MioEUR) | 788 | 754 | +4.5% |
| Employees (FTE) | 9,257 | 7,596 | +21.9% |

[Source: Extraction notes, AR 2025]

**Profile:** The profitability powerhouse. At 29.3% operating margin, W&A earns nearly triple the margin of Vehicle Systems and double that of Electronic Solutions. This segment benefits from:
- High volume production with scale economics (ammunition is consumed and reordered)
- [Massive capacity expansion investments](../concepts/ammunition-capacity-expansion.md) (EUR 402m capex in 2025 -- the highest of any segment)
- Strong pricing in a supply-constrained market

Ukraine was the largest customer for ammunition in 2025. [Source: Extraction notes]

The segment's margin trajectory has been extraordinary: from ~18% in 2017 to ~30% in 2025 Q4 peaks.

| Metric | 2025 | 2024 | Change |
|--------|------|------|--------|
| Revenue (MioEUR) | 2,504 | 1,726 | +45.1% |
| Operating Result (MioEUR) | 366 | 217 | +68.7% |
| Operating Margin (%) | 14.6 | 12.6 | +2.0pp |
| Backlog - Rheinmetall (MioEUR) | **19,580** | 7,518 | +160.5% |
| Order Intake (MioEUR) | 7,231 | 4,596 | +57.3% |
| Investment (MioEUR) | 174 | 95 | +83.2% |
| Free Cash Flow (MioEUR) | 356 | 200 | +78.0% |
| Employees (FTE) | 5,765 | 4,735 | +21.7% |

[Source: Extraction notes, AR 2025]

**Profile:** The fastest-growing segment in 2025 (+45% revenue, +69% operating result). Key 2025 programs: TaWAN LBO communication/radio system, Skyranger/Skynex air defence, SPOCK satellite program. The Rheinmetall Nomination reached a record EUR 14,235m -- more than any other segment. The backlog more than doubled, suggesting this segment's relative importance will continue growing.

Margin improvement from ~5-6% (2018) to 14.6% (2025) represents a remarkable operational transformation, driven by mix improvement, scale, and the phase-out of unprofitable legacy contracts.

## Historical Structure: Defence vs Automotive (2013-2019)

### Rheinmetall Automotive

| Year | Revenue (MioEUR) | Op. Result (MioEUR) | Margin (%) |
|------|-----------------|--------------------|----|
| 2013 | 2,262 | 158 | 7.0 |
| 2014 | 2,448 | 184 | 7.5 |
| 2015 | 2,592 | 216 | 8.3 |
| 2016 | 2,656 | 223 | 8.4 |
| 2017 | 2,861 | 249 | 8.7 |
| 2018 | 2,930 | 262 | 8.9 (record) |
| 2019 | 2,736 | 184 | 6.7 |

[Source: Extraction notes]

Automotive peaked in 2018 at EUR 2.93bn revenue and 8.9% margin. The 2019 decline was driven by diesel crisis, WLTP disruption, and China market weakness. COVID in 2020 dealt the final blow.

### Rheinmetall Defence

| Year | Revenue (MioEUR) | Op. Result (MioEUR) | Margin (%) | Order Backlog (MioEUR) |
|------|-----------------|--------------------|----|----------------------|
| 2013 | 2,155 | 60 | 2.8 | 6,050 |
| 2014 | 2,240 | -9 | -0.4 | 6,516 |
| 2015 | 2,591 | 90 | 3.5 | 6,422 |
| 2016 | 2,946 | 147 | 5.0 | 6,656 |
| 2017 | 3,036 | 174 | 5.7 | 6,416 |
| 2018 | 3,221 | 254 | 7.9 | 8,577 |
| 2019 | 3,519 | 321 | 9.1 | ~10,400 |

[Source: Extraction notes]

The defense margin journey is remarkable: from -0.4% (2014) to 9.1% (2019) to 18.5% group-level (2025). The low margins in 2013-2015 reflected legacy contracts (Dutch Boxer, German Puma) with "mid-single-digit contribution margins" versus a normal 15-20%. [Source: Transcript FY18] As these phased out and new contracts entered at better terms, margins structurally improved.

Vehicle Systems has the highest R&D spend (reflecting complex platform development), but Electronic Solutions has nearly as many R&D employees despite lower spend -- consistent with its software/systems-heavy nature.
