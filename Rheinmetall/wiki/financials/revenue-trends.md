## Revenue Trajectory: Three Phases

### Phase 1: Slow Growth (2016-2019)
Revenue grew modestly from 5.6bn to 6.3bn (+12% cumulative over 4 years). This was a period where defense and automotive segments roughly balanced each other. The foreign revenue share was high (69-77%), reflecting Rheinmetall's global customer base. Growth was incremental, driven by individual program wins.

### Phase 2: COVID Disruption (2020-2021)
The 2020 decline of -13.6% was overwhelmingly driven by the [automotive segment collapse](../concepts/automotive-to-defense-pivot.md). Defense revenue proved resilient, but the automotive division suffered severely from production shutdowns and demand destruction. Recovery in 2021 was partial -- revenue didn't regain 2019 levels.

### Phase 3: Defense Supercycle (2022-2025)
The [Ukraine invasion](../concepts/ukraine-war-impact.md) in February 2022 triggered a structural shift. Revenue grew +78% from 2021 to 2025 (5,658 to 9,935 MioEUR). The 2025 growth of +28.8% was the largest single-year increase in the dataset, reflecting massive defense order conversion.

**The declining foreign share (76% in 2023 down to 62% in 2025) is noteworthy** -- it suggests the domestic German market (Bundeswehr) became a much larger customer as Germany's EUR 100bn Sondervermoegen (special defense fund) began flowing into procurement. [Analysis]

## Cumulative Growth Comparison

| Period | Start | End | Total Growth | CAGR |
|--------|-------|-----|-------------|------|
| Pre-Ukraine (2016-2021) | 5,602 | 5,658 | +1.0% | +0.2% |
| Post-Ukraine (2021-2025) | 5,658 | 9,935 | +75.6% | +15.1% |

The contrast is stark: **near-zero growth over 5 years before Ukraine, then 75%+ growth in 4 years after.**
