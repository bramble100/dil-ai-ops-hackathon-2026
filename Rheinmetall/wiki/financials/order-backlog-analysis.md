# Order Backlog Analysis

## The EUR 63.8bn Pipeline

Rheinmetall's backlog as of 31 December 2025 stood at **63,800 MioEUR** -- representing over 6 years of revenue at current run rates. This is an extraordinary level of order visibility for any industrial company and reflects the structural [European defense spending](../concepts/european-defense-spending.md) shift.

## Order Data Evolution

### Order Intake

| Year | Order Intake (MioEUR) | Book-to-Bill Ratio |
|------|----------------------|-------------------|
| 2022 | 5,629 | 0.88x |
| 2023 | 11,843 | 1.65x |
| 2024 | 16,554 | 2.15x |
| 2025 | 19,867 | 2.00x |

[Source: AR 2025 p.3, AR 2023 p.2]

The book-to-bill ratio (order intake / revenue) has been consistently above 1.0x since 2023, meaning Rheinmetall books more new orders each year than it delivers -- the backlog keeps growing. At 2.0x in 2025, the company won almost EUR 2 in new orders for every EUR 1 it shipped.

### Rheinmetall Nomination (Broader Pipeline)

| Year | Nomination (MioEUR) | Description |
|------|--------------------|----|
| 2022 | 9,500 | First year of new order metric reporting |
| 2023 | 19,881 | +109% |
| 2024 | 18,497 | Broadly stable |
| 2025 | 26,400 | +43% |

"Rheinmetall Nomination" is a broader metric than traditional order intake. It includes framework agreements and designations that haven't yet converted into firm orders (Booked Business). This metric was introduced around 2022 to capture the new reality of multi-year defense procurement.

### Backlog Buildup

| Year | Rheinmetall Backlog (MioEUR) |
|------|----------------------------|
| 2022 | 26,572 |
| 2023 | 38,290 |
| 2024 | [Gap] |
| 2025 | 63,800 |

From 26.6bn to 63.8bn in three years -- a 2.4x increase. At 2025 revenue of 9.9bn, the backlog represents **6.4 years of revenue coverage**.

## Order Composition (2023 breakdown)

From the 2023 report, the order structure was:

| Category | 2023 (MioEUR) | 2022 (MioEUR) |
|----------|--------------|--------------|
| Booked Business | 3,480 | 3,521 |
| Frame Nomination | 7,362 | 350 |
| Frame Utilization | -2,804 | - |
| **Total Order Intake** | **11,843** | **5,629** |

[Source: AR 2023 p.2]

The explosion in "Frame Nomination" from 350 MioEUR (2022) to 7,362 MioEUR (2023) reflects the flood of new multi-year framework agreements as European governments committed to large-scale rearmament programs.

## Strategic Implications

1. **Revenue visibility is exceptional.** 6+ years of backlog provides rare certainty for capacity planning, capex decisions, and hiring.

2. **Conversion timing matters.** Large framework agreements convert to firm orders and then to deliveries over multi-year cycles. The gap between nomination and delivery can be 3-5 years for complex vehicle systems.

3. **Capacity is the constraint, not demand.** With a 2.0x book-to-bill, Rheinmetall's growth is limited by how fast it can build production capacity, not by customer demand. This explains the aggressive [ammunition capacity expansion](../concepts/ammunition-capacity-expansion.md) and new factory investments.
