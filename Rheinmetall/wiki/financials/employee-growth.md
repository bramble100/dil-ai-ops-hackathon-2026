## Key Observations

### Steady Growth Even Pre-Ukraine
Headcount grew from ~21,000 (2016) to ~25,500 (2022) -- a 21% increase over 6 years, roughly matching revenue growth. The domestic/international split remained close to 50/50.

### Defense vs Automotive Split
From 2020 onward, the reports separated continuing (defense) from discontinued (automotive) employees. The defense workforce grew from 19,500 to 21,788 (+12%) in just two years (2020-2022), while the automotive workforce steadily declined from ~3,800 to ~3,700.

### COVID Impact Was Minimal on Headcount
Total headcount only dipped from 23,780 (2019) to 23,268 (2020) -- a -2% decline. This was almost entirely in the international automotive operations. Defense employment actually grew through COVID.

### International Expansion
The near-50/50 domestic/international split reflects Rheinmetall's global footprint. Key international locations include:
- **Americas:** USA (Auburn Hills, Sterling Heights, Marinette), Canada, Brazil, Mexico
- **Europe:** UK (RBSL), Italy (Leonardo JV), Netherlands, Switzerland, Norway, Romania, Lithuania, Ukraine
- **Asia-Pacific:** Australia, South Korea, Japan, South Africa
