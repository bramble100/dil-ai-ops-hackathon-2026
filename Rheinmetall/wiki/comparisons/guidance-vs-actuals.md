# Management Guidance vs Actual Results

## Overview

[Armin Papperger](../entities/armin-papperger.md) explicitly acknowledged the pattern on the FY2020 call: "We are really conservative. We want to deliver what is going into the guidance and we want to deliver 100%. And as you know year by year, at the end of the year, usually we deliver more."

The transcript data supports this claim. Management systematically under-promised and over-delivered, particularly on defense metrics.

## Detailed Tracking

### FY2018 Guidance (given March 2018, for FY2018)

| Metric | Guidance | Actual | Beat/Miss |
|--------|----------|--------|-----------|
| Group sales growth | +8% to +9% | +4.3% (to 6,148m) | MISS (automotive drag) |
| Automotive sales growth | +3% to +4% | +4.2% (FX-adj.) | In-line |
| Defence sales growth | +12% to +14% | +6.1% (to 3,221m) | MISS (contract timing) |
| Group operating margin | ~7% | 8.0% | BEAT |
| Automotive margin | ~8.5% | 8.9% (record) | BEAT |
| Defence margin | 6% to 6.5% | 7.9% | BEAT significantly |

**Pattern:** Revenue growth disappointed (contract timing, delayed orders), but profitability beat expectations. Defence margin beat guided range by 140-190bps.

### FY2019 Guidance (given March 2019, for FY2019)

| Metric | Guidance | Actual | Beat/Miss |
|--------|----------|--------|-----------|
| Group sales growth | +4% to +6% | +1.7% (to 6,255m) | MISS |
| Automotive sales growth | 0% to +1% | -6.8% | MISS (market collapse) |

**Pattern:** Automotive underperformance dragged group figures. Defence again exceeded margin guidance.

### FY2021 Guidance (given March 2021, for FY2021)

| Metric | Guidance | Actual | Beat/Miss |
|--------|----------|--------|-----------|
| Group operational sales growth | +7% to +9% | +4.7% (to 5,658m) | MISS (but restated base) |

## Key Observations

1. **Systematic margin conservatism.** Management consistently guided margins below what they delivered, particularly in defense. This built credibility with the analyst community.

2. **Revenue timing was the weak spot.** Defense contracts are lumpy, and revenue recognition depends on complex milestone schedules. Revenue guidance was missed more often than profit guidance.

3. **Automotive blindside.** The one area where management genuinely missed was Automotive deterioration in 2019-2020. They maintained a "resilience" narrative too long. [Analysis]

4. **Target upgrades accelerated post-Ukraine.** The 2027 targets (set ~2022) were already being upgraded to 2030 targets by 2025, reflecting how dramatically the operating environment changed.
