# Ammunition Capacity Expansion

## Overview

Ammunition production has been the single most important capacity expansion story for Rheinmetall in the post-Ukraine era. The company has invested billions in scaling up munitions manufacturing to meet surging European demand.

## Why Ammunition Is Central

1. **Immediate consumption:** Ukraine's frontline consumed artillery ammunition at rates exceeding Cold War-era European stockpiles. Replenishment demand was urgent.
2. **High margins:** Weapon & Ammunition historically delivered Rheinmetall's highest margins -- reaching ~30% in Q4 2020 and consistently 15%+ division-level. [Source: Transcript FY20]
3. **Recurring revenue:** Ammunition is consumed and must be reordered, unlike vehicles that have 20-30 year lifecycles.
4. **Scale economics:** Higher production volumes spread fixed costs, improving unit economics.

## Production Capabilities

Rheinmetall produces a wide range of ammunition:
- **155mm artillery shells** -- the backbone of Ukrainian and NATO ground forces
- **120mm tank ammunition** -- for Leopard 2 and Challenger 2 (UK, new market after Challenger LEP)
- **35mm air defense ammunition** -- including airburst munitions for counter-drone/swarm defense
- **Medium caliber** (20mm, 25mm, 30mm) -- for IFV/APC platforms
- **Small arms ammunition**
- **Propellant charges and components**

### Innovation: 70+ km Range 155mm
On the FY2020 call, Papperger highlighted new 155mm ammunition capable of 70+ km range vs. the ~40 km industry standard. This represented a significant capability leap. [Source: Transcript FY20]

### Counter-Drone Systems
The Azerbaijan-Armenia conflict (2020) catalyzed new development in:
- **Loitering ammunition** -- entering the portfolio
- **Counter-drone/swarm defense** based on 35mm airburst munitions
- Papperger viewed drones as additive, not substitutive to traditional systems: tanks still needed, but counter-drone is a major growth area. [Source: Transcript FY20]

## Operational Risk Management

A revealing detail from the FY2019 call: Papperger disclosed that Rheinmetall held **one year of nitrocellulose stock** (a key propellant raw material) as deliberate risk management. "This is the reason of our risk management that we have also higher working capital in some areas because it's cheaper to have this higher working capital than not to be able to produce." [Source: Transcript FY19]

This long-term stockpiling strategy, which predated the Ukraine crisis, positioned Rheinmetall to ramp production faster than competitors when demand surged in 2022.

## New Factories

The 2025 annual report references "Investitionen fuer Kapazitaetserweiterungen" (investments for capacity expansion) as a key supervisory board discussion topic. [Source: AR 2025, p.16]

Specific mentions include:
- **Ukraine:** New production facilities built "in close coordination with foreign governments" [Source: AR 2025, p.9]
- **Lithuania:** New factory announced
- **Expansion of existing German and European sites**
