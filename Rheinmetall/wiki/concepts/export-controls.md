# Export Controls

## Overview

Rheinmetall operates under one of the world's strictest arms export control regimes. The regulatory environment is a material factor in revenue forecasting, customer strategy, and market access.

## German/EU Regulatory Framework

From the 2025 annual report:

> "Germany has one of the strictest export control systems in the world."

[Source: AR 2025, p.26]

Key legislation:
- **Grundgesetz (Basic Law)** -- constitutional constraints on arms exports
- **Kriegswaffenkontrollgesetz (KrWaffKontrG)** -- War Weapons Control Act
- **Aussenwirtschaftsgesetz (AWG)** -- Foreign Trade Act + Aussenwirtschaftsverordnung (AWV)
- **Political Principles of 26 June 2019** -- government guidelines for export approvals
- **EU Dual-Use Regulation** -- controls goods with civilian and military applications

International regimes:
- **Wassenaar Arrangement** -- conventional arms and dual-use technology
- **Arms Trade Treaty (ATT)** -- UN framework since December 2014
- **EU Common Position** -- harmonized export criteria among EU members

## Impact on Rheinmetall's Business

### Saudi Arabia
Export restrictions to Saudi Arabia were a recurring issue in the 2018-2020 period. Following the Khashoggi murder (October 2018), Germany imposed a blanket export ban on Saudi Arabia.

- Rheinmetall had a truck manufacturing license contract with Saudi Arabia
- The license was formally cancelled in December 2020
- Rheinmetall claimed damage compensation [Source: Transcript FY20]
- EUR 50 million in working capital was tied up in the dispute [Source: Transcript FY20]

### South Africa
Rheinmetall's South African subsidiary (Rheinmetall Denel Munition) faced export restrictions that cost an estimated **EUR 90 million in lost revenue in 2019 alone**, "eliminating almost 3% of growth." [Source: Transcript FY19]

On the FY2019 call, Papperger indicated "light at the end of the tunnel" with the South African government agreeing to reform export regulations. By 2020, the situation was improving but remained a drag.

### Quantified Revenue Impact
Papperger's direct statement: export restrictions cost ~EUR 90 million in 2019, affecting primarily Weapon & Ammunition margins. South Africa, Saudi Arabia, and Italy were the problem areas. [Source: Transcript FY19]

## Post-Ukraine Evolution

The war fundamentally altered the political calculus around European arms exports:
- Germany relaxed export restrictions for Ukraine deliveries
- Cross-European procurement initiatives (EU Defence Industrial Strategy) aim to streamline intra-EU defense trade
- The political stigma around defense spending and arms exports diminished significantly

However, the underlying legal framework remains intact, and exports to non-NATO/non-EU countries still require case-by-case approval.

## US Tariffs and Trade Policy (2025)

The 2025 annual report also notes a new trade dimension: US tariffs on EU-origin goods effective August 2025. While primarily affecting the automotive segment (Power Systems), this adds complexity to Rheinmetall's US defense business. [Source: AR 2025, p.28]
