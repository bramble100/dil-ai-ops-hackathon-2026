# Digitalization and AI

## Electronic Solutions Division

The Electronic Solutions division represents Rheinmetall's "digital backbone" -- encompassing the entire networked kill chain in protected multi-domain operations. From the 2025 annual report (translated):

> "From AI-powered sensors through platforms to (semi-)automated connection of effectors, a common operational picture emerges in the holistic system network -- shielded against attacks from cyberspace."

[Source: AR 2025, p.24]

## Capabilities

### C4ISR (Command, Control, Communications, Computers, Intelligence, Surveillance, Reconnaissance)
- Sensor systems for battlefield awareness
- Network-centric warfare solutions
- Integrated command and control platforms

### Cyber Defense
- Protection of military communications and data networks
- Defensive cyber operations

### Simulation and Training
- Training systems for all armed forces branches
- Operator solutions for aircraft
- Digital twin technologies

### Autonomous Systems
- R&D investment in unmanned ground vehicles
- AI/autonomous vehicle capabilities mentioned in FY2020 R&D ramp (24% increase to EUR 180 million) [Source: Transcript FY20]

## R&D and Innovation

### Rheinmetall Technology Center (RTC)
The "Cluster New Technologies" comprises:
- **Rheinmetall Technology Center GmbH (RTC)** -- cross-divisional technology development
- **MIRA GmbH** -- advanced technology
- **Yardstick Robotics GmbH** -- robotics

The RTC focuses on "innovative technologies and products, also outside the existing product portfolio," building first functional samples and prototypes, then transferring to commercial divisions. [Source: AR 2025, p.30]

### Key Technology Areas
- High energy laser systems (mentioned in FY2020 R&D)
- AI for sensor fusion and target acquisition
- 130mm gun development (next-generation tank armament, potential MGCS weapon)
- Counter-drone/swarm systems leveraging AI targeting
- Digital battlefield management

## Financial Profile

Electronic Solutions has historically had lower margins than Weapon & Ammunition but higher growth potential:

| Period | Electronic Solutions Margin | Notes |
|--------|---------------------------|-------|
| FY2017 | Low single digits | Burdened by strategic project preparation costs |
| FY2018 Q4 | Improved significantly | Q4 sales +40%, result +84% |
| FY2019 | ~5-6% | Growing steadily |
| FY2020 | ~8-10% | Improved mix; midterm target 8% |

Management's midterm margin target was 7-8% (stated FY2018 call), later raised to minimum 10% hurdle for all divisions (FY2020 call). [Source: Transcripts FY18, FY20]
