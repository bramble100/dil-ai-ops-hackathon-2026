# Amszterdam latnivalok: amiket ne hagyj ki [2026-ban]

**Source:** Netkoffer.hu (Hungarian travel site)
**Language:** Hungarian
**Updated:** 2026-02-17

## Summary

A well-structured Hungarian-language guide to Amsterdam's must-see attractions, updated February 2026. Particularly strong on transport details, city passes, and pricing. Also includes day trip suggestions to Zaanse Schans and Giethoorn.

## Transport Details

Amsterdam's public transport network consists of:
- **14 tram lines**
- **30+ bus lines**
- **7 ferry routes**
- **5 metro lines**

## Sights & Attractions Covered

### Major Landmarks
- **Dam Square** - central hub
- **Royal Palace** - on Dam Square
- **Begijnhof** - medieval courtyard
- **Montelbaan Tower** - historic defense tower

### Museums
- **Van Gogh Museum**
- **Rijksmuseum**
- **Rembrandt House Museum**
- **Anne Frank House**
- **Het Scheepvaartmuseum (Maritime Museum)**
- **NEMO Science Museum**
- **Houseboat Museum** - entry EUR 9.50
- **Madame Tussauds**

### Churches
- **Oude Kerk (Old Church)** - entry EUR 14.50

### Markets & Shopping
- **Bloemenmarkt** - floating flower market
- **Albert Cuyp Market** - largest street market in Amsterdam

### Experiences
- **Heineken Experience** - brewery tour
- **Diamond workshops** - traditional Amsterdam craft
- **Canal cruises**

### Parks & Nature
- **Vondelpark**
- **Artis Zoo**

### Day Trips
- **Zaanse Schans** - windmill village
- **Giethoorn** - "Venice of the North"

## Key Takeaways

- The I Amsterdam City Card can offer significant savings if visiting 3+ museums per day (break-even analysis favors multi-day cards)
- Transport passes are essential -- individual GVB tickets are expensive by comparison
- The Houseboat Museum (EUR 9.50) is one of the cheapest unique attractions
- Oude Kerk at EUR 14.50 is notable as a paid church entry
- February 2026 update makes pricing data highly reliable for current trip planning
- Both Zaanse Schans and Giethoorn are recommended as day trips from Amsterdam
