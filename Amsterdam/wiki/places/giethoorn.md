# Giethoorn

Often called the "Venice of the Netherlands," Giethoorn is a canal village located approximately 120 km northeast of Amsterdam. The village is largely car-free, with residents and visitors navigating its waterways by small motorboat. [Multi-source]

## Overview

Giethoorn is defined by its canals lined with traditional Dutch houses, small wooden bridges, and lush gardens. The absence of cars in much of the village center creates a uniquely peaceful and atmospheric setting. Gliding through the canals by boat, past thatched-roof farmhouses and under arched bridges, is the quintessential Giethoorn experience. [Source: viajeros-callejeros]

## What to Do

- **Rent a small motorboat** (known locally as a "whisper boat") and navigate the canals at your own pace [Source: viajeros-callejeros]
- **Walk or cycle** along the canal-side paths [Source: viajeros-callejeros]
- **Enjoy the atmosphere** - Giethoorn is as much about the ambience as specific sights [Source: viajeros-callejeros]

## Getting There

Giethoorn is 120 km from Amsterdam, making it one of the longer day trips. Options include: [Multi-source]

- **By car** - the most flexible option [Unverified]
- **By bus tour** - organized bus excursions from Amsterdam are available; Hungarian sources specifically recommend these [Source: amszterdam-latnivalok]
