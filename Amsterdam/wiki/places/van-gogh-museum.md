# Van Gogh Museum

The Van Gogh Museum holds the world's largest collection of works by Vincent van Gogh, comprising more than 200 paintings, 500 drawings, and 700 letters. It is one of Amsterdam's most visited attractions, located at Museumplein alongside the [Rijksmuseum](rijksmuseum.md) and near [Vondelpark](vondelpark.md). [Multi-source]

## Collection

The permanent collection is organized chronologically into five periods corresponding to the places where Van Gogh lived and worked:

1. **Holland** (1880--1885) -- early dark-toned works including peasant scenes
2. **Paris** (1886--1888) -- influence of Impressionism, lighter palette
3. **Arles** (1888--1889) -- iconic Sunflowers, The Yellow House, vibrant color
4. **Saint-Remy** (1889--1890) -- asylum period, Starry Night era
5. **Auvers-sur-Oise** (1890) -- final prolific weeks before his death

[Source: amszterdam-latnivalok]

The Van Gogh collection is supplemented by works from contemporary artists who influenced or were influenced by Van Gogh, providing context for his artistic development. [Source: amszterdam-latnivalok]

## Friday Evening Events

During peak season, the museum hosts special Friday evening events featuring cocktails and a DJ, transforming the gallery into an atmospheric social venue. [Source: amszterdam-latnivalok]

## Tips

- The museum can get very crowded; early morning or late afternoon visits tend to be quieter. [Unverified]
- If visiting both the Van Gogh Museum and the [Rijksmuseum](rijksmuseum.md), plan for a full day at Museumplein. [Unverified]
- The Friday evening events are a unique way to experience the museum -- check the official website for dates and availability. [Source: amszterdam-latnivalok]
