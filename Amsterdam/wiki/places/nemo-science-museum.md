# NEMO Science Museum

NEMO is Amsterdam's interactive science museum, housed in a striking ship-shaped five-story building designed by Italian architect **Renzo Piano**. The building's distinctive green copper hull rises from the waterfront like the prow of an enormous vessel. It is one of the city's best destinations for families and anyone with a curious mind. [Multi-source]

## Exhibits by Floor

The museum is organized across five floors, each with a distinct theme:

| Floor | Theme | Highlights |
|---|---|---|
| **Floor 1** | Life & Biology | DNA exploration, chain reactions |
| **Floor 2** | Technology & Engineering | Ball factory -- sorting balls by size, color, and weight |
| **Floor 3** | Science Lab | Hands-on laboratory experiments |
| **Floor 4** | The Human Mind | Brain tests and cognitive challenges |
| **Floor 5** | Rooftop Terrace | City panorama -- **free access, no ticket needed** |

[Source: amszterdam-latnivalok]

All exhibits are interactive and designed to engage both children and adults. The hands-on approach makes abstract scientific concepts tangible and fun. [Multi-source]

## Rooftop Terrace

The rooftop terrace on Floor 5 deserves special mention: it offers one of the best panoramic views of Amsterdam's skyline and the [Canal Ring](canal-ring.md). Crucially, **access to the rooftop is free** -- you do not need a museum ticket. It also features a wading pool in summer and a cafe. [Source: amszterdam-latnivalok]

## Tips

- Even if you do not plan to visit the museum itself, the free rooftop terrace is worth the trip for the views alone. [Source: amszterdam-latnivalok]
- NEMO is especially well suited for children, but the exhibits are genuinely engaging for adults too. [Multi-source]
- The building is located near Amsterdam Centraal station, making it easy to combine with a walk along the waterfront. [Unverified]
