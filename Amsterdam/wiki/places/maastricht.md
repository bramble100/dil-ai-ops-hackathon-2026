# Maastricht

The southernmost major city in the Netherlands, located 210 km southeast of Amsterdam. Considered by many to be the oldest city in the Netherlands, Maastricht has a distinctly different character from the cities of Holland, with strong Belgian and French cultural influences. [Source: viajeros-callejeros]

## Overview

Maastricht is known for its Burgundian culture - a term the Dutch use to describe an appreciation for good food, drink, and the pleasures of life. Situated in the narrow strip of the Netherlands that extends south between Belgium and Germany, the city feels markedly different from Amsterdam, Utrecht, or any of the Randstad cities. The architecture, cuisine, dialect, and general atmosphere all reflect the cross-border influences of its location. [Source: viajeros-callejeros]

## Character

- **Burgundian lifestyle** - Maastricht embraces fine dining, cafe culture, and a relaxed pace of life more reminiscent of Belgium or France than of the pragmatic north [Source: viajeros-callejeros]
- **Oldest city claim** - frequently cited as the Netherlands' oldest city, with a history stretching back to Roman times [Source: viajeros-callejeros]
- **Cultural crossroads** - the Belgian and French influence is palpable in everything from the local cuisine to the architecture [Source: viajeros-callejeros]

## Getting There

Maastricht is 210 km from Amsterdam. Due to the distance, this is one of the longer day trips and may be better suited as an overnight stay. Train services run from Amsterdam to Maastricht. [Source: viajeros-callejeros]
