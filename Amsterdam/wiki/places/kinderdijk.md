# Kinderdijk

A UNESCO World Heritage Site featuring 19 historic windmills, located south of Rotterdam. Kinderdijk represents the largest concentration of historic windmills in the Netherlands and stands as a monument to the Dutch centuries-long battle against the sea. [Source: viajeros-callejeros]

## Overview

The 19 windmills of Kinderdijk were built for water management, pumping excess water from the low-lying polders to prevent flooding. They are an iconic symbol of Dutch engineering ingenuity and the nation's enduring relationship with water. The site is also a stunning photography destination, especially at sunrise or sunset when the windmills are silhouetted against the sky. [Source: viajeros-callejeros]

## What to See and Do

- **19 windmills** - walk or cycle along the paths connecting the windmills for a full appreciation of the landscape [Source: viajeros-callejeros]
- **Two windmill museums** - two of the windmills are open to visitors as museums, allowing you to see the interior workings and learn about the millers who lived and worked inside them [Source: viajeros-callejeros]

## Getting There

### From Rotterdam
Kinderdijk can be reached by boat or bus from Rotterdam. [Source: viajeros-callejeros]

### From Amsterdam
The journey from Amsterdam takes 2 or more hours by public transport, typically requiring a transfer in Rotterdam. A car is recommended for the most convenient visit. [Source: viajeros-callejeros]
