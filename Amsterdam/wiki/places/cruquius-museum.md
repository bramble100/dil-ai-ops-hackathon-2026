# Museum De Cruquius

A museum housed in the historic Cruquius steam pumping station, located at Cruquiusdijk 27, 2142 ER Cruquius (52.33806N, 4.63833E). The museum tells the story of the Dutch battle against water and preserves what is thought to be the largest steam engine ever built. [Source: cruquius-wikipedia]

## History

The pumping station is named after Nicolaas Kruik (1678-1754), a land surveyor. It was one of three steam-powered stations - alongside the Leeghwater and Lynden stations - that drained the Haarlemmermeer, a large inland lake, beginning in 1850. The three stations accomplished this enormous feat in just three years. [Source: cruquius-wikipedia]

The building has served as a museum since 1933. [Source: cruquius-wikipedia]

## The Steam Engine

The engine at Cruquius was built by **Harvey & Co of Hayle, Cornwall**, England. It is a compound steam engine with: [Source: cruquius-wikipedia]

- **Inner high-pressure cylinder:** 84 inches (2.1 m) diameter
- **Outer low-pressure annular cylinder:** 144 inches (3.7 m) diameter

This engine is thought to be the **largest steam engine ever built**, and is certainly the largest beam engine in existence. [Source: cruquius-wikipedia]

## Official Status

The Cruquius pumping station holds multiple heritage designations: [Source: cruquius-wikipedia]

- **Rijksmonument** - Dutch national heritage site
- **ERIH Anchor Point** - European Route of Industrial Heritage
- **ASME Historic Mechanical Engineering Landmark** (designated 1991) - American Society of Mechanical Engineers

## Stelling van Amsterdam Context

The museum is situated in the context of the Stelling van Amsterdam (Defence Line of Amsterdam). Nearby related sites include: [Source: cruquius-wikipedia]

- **Fort Vijfhuizen** - World Heritage site
- **Fort Cruquius** - World Heritage site
