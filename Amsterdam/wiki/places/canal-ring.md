# Canal Ring (Grachtengordel)

Amsterdam's canal system has been a **UNESCO World Heritage Site since 2010**. The city has 165 canals totaling over 100 km of waterways, crossed by 1,281 bridges -- more than three times the number in Venice. The canal network is the defining feature of Amsterdam's cityscape and one of the most recognizable urban landscapes in the world. [Multi-source]

## The Four Main Canals

The canal ring consists of four concentric semicircular canals, built outward from the medieval city center:

1. **Singel** -- the innermost canal, originally a medieval moat; today home to the [Bloemenmarkt](bloemenmarkt.md) (floating flower market) [Multi-source]
2. **Herengracht** (Gentlemen's Canal) -- the most prestigious address; grand merchant houses line its banks [Source: amszterdam-latnivalok]
3. **Keizersgracht** (Emperor's Canal) -- the widest of the main canals [Source: amszterdam-latnivalok]
4. **Prinsengracht** (Prince's Canal) -- the outermost ring; home to the [Anne Frank House](anne-frank-house.md) and the Westerkerk [Multi-source]

## History

The canals were built primarily during the **17th century** (the Dutch Golden Age). Originally constructed for **defense**, they quickly evolved to serve **trade and transport**, with merchant warehouses lining the banks. The canal ring represented a bold feat of urban planning that turned marshy terrain into one of Europe's wealthiest cities. [Multi-source]

## Houseboats

One of Amsterdam's most distinctive features is its houseboats. Locals live on them year-round, and some are available as rentals for visitors. [Source: amszterdam-latnivalok]

A particularly charming example is **De Poezenboot** (the Cat Boat) -- a floating cat shelter on the Singel canal that takes in stray and abandoned cats. [Source: amszterdam-latnivalok]

## Canal Cruises

Canal cruises are one of the most popular tourist activities in Amsterdam, offering views of the canal houses, bridges, and houseboats from the water. Multiple operators run tours from various departure points throughout the city. [Multi-source]

## Fun Fact

An estimated **25,000 bicycles** end up in Amsterdam's canals every year. The city regularly dredges them out -- a testament to just how central both canals and cycling are to Amsterdam life. [Source: amszterdam-latnivalok]

## Tips

- A canal cruise is an excellent way to get an overview of the city, especially on a first visit. [Multi-source]
- Walking or cycling along the canals in the evening, when the canal houses are illuminated, is one of Amsterdam's great pleasures. [Unverified]
- The canal ring connects many of Amsterdam's key attractions, including [Dam Square](dam-square.md), the [Anne Frank House](anne-frank-house.md), and the [Bloemenmarkt](bloemenmarkt.md). [Multi-source]
