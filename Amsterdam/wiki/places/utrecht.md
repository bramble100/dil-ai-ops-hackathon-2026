# Utrecht

A vibrant city located 45 km southeast of Amsterdam, in the geographic heart of the Netherlands. Utrecht claims to have the "most beautiful canals in Europe" and holds the highest bicycle-per-capita ratio in the country. [Source: viajeros-callejeros]

## Overview

Utrecht blends a well-preserved historic old town with a modern urban landscape. Its unique wharf-level canal cellars, now home to restaurants and terraces right at the water's edge, distinguish it from every other Dutch canal city. It is a university city with a youthful energy. [Source: viajeros-callejeros]

## Key Sights

- **Dom Tower** - the tallest church tower in the Netherlands; climb it for commanding views over the city and surrounding countryside [Source: viajeros-callejeros]
- **DOMunder** - an underground tour beneath the Dom Square that takes visitors through layers of Utrecht's history [Source: viajeros-callejeros]
- **Flower market** - a traditional open-air market [Source: viajeros-callejeros]
- **Textile market** - another of Utrecht's characteristic markets [Source: viajeros-callejeros]
- **Churches, parks, and museums** - the compact old town is rich with historic churches, green spaces, and cultural institutions [Source: viajeros-callejeros]

## Getting There

Take an intercity train from Amsterdam Centraal to Utrecht Centraal. The journey takes under 30 minutes, making this one of the quickest and easiest day trips from Amsterdam. [Source: viajeros-callejeros]
