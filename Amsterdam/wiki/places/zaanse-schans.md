# Zaanse Schans

An open-air windmill village located 20 km north of Amsterdam. Zaanse Schans functions like an outdoor museum of 17th and 18th century Dutch life, offering a window into the country's industrial and cultural heritage. [Source: viajeros-callejeros]

## Overview

The village preserves and presents traditional Dutch crafts, architecture, and industry in an authentic riverside setting. It is one of the most popular day trips from Amsterdam and is recommended across multiple travel sources. [Multi-source]

## What to See and Do

- **Working windmills** - several historic windmills are still operational, and some can be entered and explored from the inside [Source: viajeros-callejeros]
- **Cheese farm** - watch traditional Dutch cheese being made and sample the results [Source: viajeros-callejeros]
- **Clog factory** - see how traditional Dutch wooden shoes (klompen) are crafted; clogs make excellent souvenirs [Source: viajeros-callejeros]

## Getting There

### By Train
Take a train from Amsterdam Centraal to **Koog-Zaandijk** station. The journey takes approximately 15 minutes. From the station, it is a 10-minute walk to Zaanse Schans, crossing a drawbridge along the way. [Source: viajeros-callejeros]

### By Bus Excursion
Organized bus excursions are available from Amsterdam, including options with Spanish-speaking guides. [Source: viajeros-callejeros]
