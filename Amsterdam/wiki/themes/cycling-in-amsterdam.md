# Cycling in Amsterdam

## Overview

Amsterdam is one of the world's premier cycling cities. Bicycles outnumber people, and the flat terrain combined with extensive bike lane infrastructure makes cycling the fastest and most enjoyable way to explore the city. **[Multi-source]**

## Key Facts

- Hundreds of kilometers of dedicated bike lanes (fietspaden)
- ~25,000 bicycles end up in canals each year **[Source: top10-amszterdam]**
- [Utrecht](../places/utrecht.md) claims the highest bike-to-resident ratio in the Netherlands **[Source: viajeros-callejeros]**
- The [Rijksmuseum](../places/rijksmuseum.md) is the only museum in the world with a cycling tunnel underneath it **[Source: top10-amszterdam]**

## For Visitors

Bike rental is widely available throughout Amsterdam and at tourist destinations like [Keukenhof](../places/keukenhof.md). When cycling:

- Stay in bike lanes (marked red or separate from car lanes)
- Signal turns with your hand
- Watch for trams -- their tracks can catch bike wheels
- Lock your bike securely (theft is common)
- Don't stop in bike lanes for photos

## Cycling Day Trips

The flat Dutch landscape is ideal for cycling excursions:
- [Zaanse Schans](../places/zaanse-schans.md) -- reachable by bike from Amsterdam
- [Volendam & Marken](../places/volendam-marken.md) -- scenic rural cycling route
- Waterland -- pastoral countryside just north of Amsterdam
